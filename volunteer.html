Volunteer • Pilot Cohort • Fall 2026 • Bay Area • 3-5 only

Volunteer-led, foundation-supported.
We equip 3-5 neighbors to lead practice for modern workers.

We are a private family foundation. Alan & Jacqueline do not have capacity to lead every hike. For Fall 2026, we are recruiting Pilot cohort: 3-5 Hike Leaders in Bay Area and 2-3 Content Translators (science → worker-friendly kit). After pilot, we expand to students & littles.

Role A • Pilot 3-5 • Bay Area

Hike Leader — Volunteer-Led Trails

Who we equip to serve: Contemporary workers — first-gen professionals, immigrant tech workers, young parents juggling work — who lack accessible mental health literacy + practice. Not therapy.

  • Commitment pilot: 1x/month, second Saturday, 2–2.5h, Fall 2026 (Sep-Nov = 3 hikes). Small group 6 max, not 12.
  • Training (2h mandatory): 1h video + 1h trail shadowing. Content: safety (heat, parking, poison oak, coyote, dogs), Leave No Trace, basic co-regulation basics (box breathing 4-4-4-4, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding), waiver & boundaries, first-aid checklist, emergency protocol, educational framing (not therapy, not diagnosis), culturally competent language for first-gen.

    Red flags & referral — we do NOT diagnose, we do NOT counsel: Volunteers learn to recognize signs that need professional care and refer out, not handle:
    — Expressions of self-harm, suicidal ideation, plan, or intent → stop, listen non-judgmentally, encourage 988 / 400-161-9995 / ER, provide crisis card, do not counsel, report to foundation safety lead.
    — Severe symptoms >2 weeks (persistent low mood, inability to function at work/home, psychosis, mania) → provide education handout + list of low-cost clinics, encourage professional evaluation.
    — Our role is strictly education + peer walk: we share free kits, we walk together, we create belonging. We do not provide therapy, counseling, diagnosis, treatment, or crisis intervention. All volunteer scripts use "I" statements and educational language: "Research shows...", "Some people find...".
  • Foundation provides: Trail cards (3 trails: Rancho San Antonio, Presidio Ridge, Redwood Regional), safety checklist, hormone micro-lesson cards (cortisol, serotonin), tea check-in script, educational waiver template, volunteer private Slack, insurance guidance note.
  • You bring: Bay Area resident, comfortable leading 5-8mi moderate, first-aid/CPR nice but not required, lived experience with work stress / first-gen helpful.
Why 3-5 not more?

We are family foundation, no staff. 3-5 allows real training, safety checks, debriefs. We prefer quality, learning, then expansion to Title I schools Phase 2.

Role B • 2-3 • Remote friendly

Content Translator — Science → Free Kit

Mission: Turn one research paper per month into 1-page worker-relevant explainer + future kid page. For underserved workers who lack access.

  • Commitment: 1x/month, 2-3h, Sep-Nov pilot.
  • Process: We assign paper (e.g., Bratman 2015, cortisol meta), you write plain-English: TL;DR, mechanism, 3 practical steps for workers, citation. No hype, no supplements. Bilingual Chinese nice.
  • Audience primary: Modern workers (first-gen, immigrant, young parents) — culturally competent, shame-free. Phase 2: Title I + low-income 3-8.
  • Foundation provides: Template, source PDFs, review, publishing to Journal, credit.

Training & liability
  • 2h training mandatory before first hike. Covers: safety, LNT, waiver collection, co-regulation basics (box breathing 4-4-4-4, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding), boundaries (no medical advice, no counseling, educational only), plus red-flag recognition & referral out: how to spot self-harm language, severe functional impairment, and refer to 988 / 400-161-9995 / low-cost clinic list — we do NOT diagnose, counsel, or handle crisis; we provide peer walk + education handout and contact safety lead.
  • Liability: Volunteers are not employees. Activity is educational, not therapy, not medical, not crisis. All participants sign educational waiver: acknowledges risks (uneven terrain, heat, wildlife), voluntary, assumes risk, not relying on medical.
  • Insurance: We carry general liability as private foundation and provide event guidelines, but leaders advised personal liability awareness. Details in training.
  • Private foundation model: Family-funded, no public solicitation. Volunteer time is donation of time.
⚠️ Educational Only — Not Medical Advice / 仅教育用途

Educational purposes only, not medical advice, not therapy, not a substitute for professional care. We provide preventive mental health literacy education + practical opportunities — 科普教育资源、实践机会、预防性教育 — not treatment, counseling, or clinical intervention. Severe cases need professional treatment. If you are in crisis: call 988 (US) / 400-161-9995 (CN) / 116 123 (UK Samaritans) or go to ER.
本项目仅提供心理健康素养教育资料与实践机会，不是治疗服务，不能替代专业医疗或心理咨询。严重情况请寻求专业治疗。

Who pilot serves — education equality lens

Primary: Modern workers — first-gen professionals, immigrant tech workers, young parents — educationally underserved in mental health literacy. They get performance reviews, not regulation education. They get parenting ads, not free, evidence-based, culturally competent kits. Our free education + volunteer practice (walk, trail, breathing) closes that gap, aligned with IRS Education Equality purpose. Phase 2: Title I students, low-income families 3-8 — same model after worker playbook.

Educational purpose only, not therapy. For crisis, 988 US.
