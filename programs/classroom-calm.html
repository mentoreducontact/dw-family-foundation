Program 03 / Education • 课堂宁静计划 • Grades 6-12

Classroom Calm
Test prep without terror. Focus without grinding.

Student stress (学生压力) is not laziness. Exam anxiety (考试焦虑) is not lack of effort. Classroom Calm gives middle and high schoolers a toolkit that fits in a pencil case: breath, grounding, and study rhythms that respect the nervous system.

Free toolkit for teachers45-min workshopEvidence-informed • 科学为基础Bilingual slides EN/中文
WHO • 为谁

Students 11-18, teachers, parents

Grades 6-12 experiencing exam stress, perfectionism (完美主义), or pre-test freeze. Also for teachers who hold the room's nervous system. We partner with public schools in Seattle, Bellevue, Renton — priority Title I. No diagnosis needed.

WHAT • 提供什么

Exam anxiety toolkit + routine

Three core practices: box breathing (箱式呼吸), 5-4-3-2-1 grounding (五感接地), Pomodoro with movement (番茄工作法+运动). Printed as a zine, taught in 45-min. Low tech: paper, timer, wall poster. Practice 2-min daily, not 20-min when panicking.

SCIENCE • 科学

Why calm helps recall

High arousal narrows working memory — Yerkes-Dodson (叶克斯-多德森定律). Slow breathing increases parasympathetic tone, lowers heart rate ~10-15%. Grounding shifts from catastrophizing to sensory present. Movement breaks raise BDNF, support memory consolidation (记忆巩固).

The problem we're actually solving (真正的问题)

Students tell us: "I studied but blanked." That's not knowledge gap, it's state-dependent retrieval. Under threat, amygdala hijacks hippocampus. The body is trying to protect you, not betray you. Our job is to give the next generation a language: "I'm activated (我被激活了), not failing."

We avoid pathologizing. Exam anxiety affects 25-40% of teens at clinically significant levels, but most never get skills — they get "just relax." Classroom Calm replaces vague advice with explicit, rehearsable micro-skills that work in 60 seconds before a test, without leaving your seat or looking weird.

Three tools, taught with care

TOOL 01 / BOX BREATHING • 箱式呼吸 • 60-90 sec

4-4-4-4: inhale, hold, exhale, hold

How: Feet flat, shoulders down. Inhale nose 4 counts, hold 4, exhale mouth 4, hold 4. Two rounds. Optional: trace a square on desk with finger — tactile anchor.
When: Before exam distribution, when heart pounds, between sections.
Science: Extended exhale and breath-hold increase CO2 tolerance, stimulate vagus (迷走神经), raise HRV. Visual: your prefrontal cortex coming back online.
Teacher cue: "Let's do our square together — not to relax, to arrive (不是为了放松，而是为了回到当下)."
文化注: 呼吸 is both breath and inspiration in Chinese — we borrow that.

TOOL 02 / 5-4-3-2-1 GROUNDING • 五感接地法

From head to senses

How: Silent scan: 5 things you see (看到), 4 you can touch (触摸), 3 you hear (听见), 2 you smell (闻到), 1 you taste (尝到). Write as acronym on exam cover: "5S-4T-3H-2S-1T".
When: Blank mind, catastrophic thoughts, comparing to peers.
Why it works: Grounding is attentional redeployment — moves activity from default mode network rumination to sensory cortex. Non-verbal, no disclosure needed.
Adaptation: For neurodivergent students we allow object hold (small stone, 石头) as tactile anchor. Classroom version: wall poster with icons.

TOOL 03 / POMODORO WITH MOVEMENT • 番茄+运动

25-5-30: work, move, repeat

Standard: 25 min focused work, 5 min movement (not phone), after 4 cycles 20-30 min longer break. Our twist: movement must be physical and opposite posture — standing desk, wall push-ups, shoulder rolls, stair walk, shake-outs.
Study science: Attention decays after ~25 min for novices. Micro-movements increase blood flow ~15%, release norepinephrine modestly to restore vigilance. Also teaches self-compassion: timer rings, you must stop, even if incomplete — training tolerance for unfinished.
Homework version: Print tracker with three columns: intention (意图), distraction (分心), return (回到). We normalize distraction as data.

Workshop format (工作坊形式)

45-min classroom workshop: 8 min science story (why brains blank), 10 min demo/ practice box breathing, 10 min 5-4-3-2-1 in pairs, 10 min Pomodoro planning, 7 min Q&A and zine handout. No phones, no recording. Teacher gets follow-up one-pager with scripts.

Materials (all free CC-BY): Student zine (8-page A5, EN/中文双语), poster 11x17" for wall, teacher cue card with 5 scripts, audio guide 4-min MP3 for practice at home. All designed quiet luxury: ivory paper, Cormorant headers, not cartoon.

We do not collect student personal data. Headcount and anonymous pre/post "how calm do you feel right now 1-5" only. Schools keep own surveys if they want. Privacy-first (隐私优先).

TEACHER POCKET SCRIPTS • 教师口袋话术

Say less, regulate more

Instead of "Calm down (冷静)", try: "Let's arrive together — feet, breath, square." Instead of "Focus", try: "Notice where attention went, invite it back (注意力去哪了，请它回来)." Instead of "Don't be anxious", try: "Activation is information (紧张是信息). We have tools."

We teach co-regulation: teacher regulates first, then room. 2 slow breaths before speaking to class reduces collective arousal measurably. This is not soft — this is classroom management via neuroscience.

What we don't do

We don't diagnose, we don't provide therapy (不提供心理治疗), we don't replace school counselors (学校心理辅导员). We are a Tier 1 universal prevention tool, not intervention. If a student discloses severe distress, we follow school's referral protocol. All facilitators have background checks, trauma-informed training, and Mandated Reporter briefing.

Bilingual hinting matters: many Asian-American families carry extra academic pressure + mental health stigma (耻感). We normalize stress as physiological, not moral failure — "压力是身体的，不是人品的." Materials show both languages not as translation but as belonging.
