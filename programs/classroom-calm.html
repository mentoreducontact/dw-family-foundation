Classroom Calm
Exam stress is a body problem first.

Middle school to college — finals, APs, SATs, college apps, Bay Area pressure is real. We give teachers and students a 5-minute toolkit they can use without leaving the desk: box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, movement breaks, sleep anchors.

Free kits • 3 pilot Bay Area schoolsNo app • Printable cardsTeacher 15-min training
WHO

Students + teachers

Students 12-24 in high-pressure environments. Especially transition years: middle to high, high to college, first internship. Bay Area public and charter schools.

KIT

5 tools, 1 poster

Box breathing card, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding card, movement break dice (30 sec), sleep hygiene mini-sheet, feelings-to-needs list. Designed cream + forest, low ink.

EVIDENCE

Physiology first, cognition second

Slow exhale extends parasympathetic activation. Grounding interrupts rumination. Brief movement restores attention. Sleep regularity predicts test performance more than extra cramming.

Toolkit detail

Box Breathing 4-4-4-4: Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4, repeat 4 times. Before test, low visibility. Can do with hand under desk.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding: Name 5 sees, 4 touches, 3 hears, 2 smells, 1 tastes. Moves brain from future catastrophizing to present senses. Works in Golden Gate Park and in exam hall.

Movement Breaks: 60 sec: neck rolls, shoulder shrugs, standing calf raises, shake hands. No need to leave room. Pomodoro 25-5 adapted to 50-10 for teens.

Sleep Anchor: Same wake time +/- 45 min, even during finals, morning light 10 min, no screens 30 min before bed, 4-7-8 wind-down. We provide parent letter in English only — plain language.

For teachers

15-minute training: how to introduce without singling out anxious students, how to model, where to put poster (side wall, not front), how to use without losing instructional time. We do not collect student data. Teacher reports anonymous aggregate: how many used, did it help — optional.

WHAT WE DO NOT DO

Not counseling, not screening, not diagnosing

We are educational only. We do not screen for anxiety or depression. We provide crisis poster with 988 and campus resources. If a student discloses safety concerns, teachers follow mandatory reporting and school protocol — we train that boundary.
