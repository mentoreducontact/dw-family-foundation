Science & Stories
We read papers so you do not have to.

Popular science for real life in the Bay Area: how movement regulates hormones, why hiking quiets rumination, how to talk to a 4-year-old about anger without shaming. Every article cites primary sources, states limitations, and gives 3 things you can try tomorrow. No supplements, no hacks, no pseudoscience.

Bilingual drafts to English only • Now pure EnglishAPA, JAMA, Stanford citations3 practical tipsEducational only
HOW WE WRITE

Plain language, real citations

We start with question from Bay Area families: “Does running help sleep?” Then find meta-analyses and systematic reviews. Explain HPA axis like a thermostat, BDNF like fertilizer. Link DOI. Note sample sizes, who was studied, who was not.

WHAT WE AVOID

No miracle cures

No “one trick fixes anxiety.” No affiliate links. No product placement. No extrapolating mouse studies to humans without saying mouse. We disclose funding: 100% family-funded Y1, 501(c)(3) public charity.

Recent topics

Move & Mood: Zone 2, endorphin, dopamine, serotonin, BDNF, cortisol 15-25% dip — explained without jargon. With Bay Area route suggestions: Presidio, Golden Gate Park.
Trail & Restoration: Attention Restoration Theory, fractal fascination in redwoods, how 20 min in park helps focus for 2 hours.
Early Emotional Literacy: Naming feelings to tame amygdala, co-regulation vs control, why validation is not permissiveness.
Work & Recovery: Burnout definition WHO, recovery experiences framework — detachment, relaxation, mastery, control.

Process

1. Community question from Bay Area — hike, run, classroom, parent night. 2. Lit search — PubMed, PsycINFO, prioritized systematic reviews and meta-analyses. 3. Draft 800 words plain English. 4. Peer review by volunteer scientist. 5. Publish free at /articles.html.

All articles include: question, mechanism, evidence summary with limitations, 3 Bay Area-friendly actions (e.g., walk Presidio Lovers Lane 30 min, 3x week), when to seek professional support, crisis 988.
