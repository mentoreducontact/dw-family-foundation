Science & Stories
Putting peer review into parent language.

A popular science hub (科普中心) that translates research into stories families can actually use. We sit between PubMed and playground bench. No hype, no miracle cures — just careful translation (严谨翻译), quiet citations, and Chinese hints where meaning deepens.

WHO • 为谁

Curious parents, teachers, new science readers

Adults who want evidence but not jargon. First-gen immigrant families who get parenting advice in two languages that contradict. Early-career researchers who want to practice translation. High schoolers who think science is "too hard" — we make it human (把科学讲成人话).

WHAT • 做什么

Essays, explainers, story-led research briefs

600-800 word essays linking daily family moments to one research idea. Format: Story → What study actually found (研究发现) → What it doesn't mean → One small try-at-home (微小尝试). Plus bilingual glossary (双语词汇表) and visual explainers. 2 stories/month.

SCIENCE • 标准

How we translate responsibly

Principles: cite primary source, distinguish correlation vs causation (相关 vs 因果), disclose effect size, include limitations (局限性). We never say "study proves." We say "suggests, with X sample." Inspired by Cochrane plain language, but with family voice.

Why another science blog? (为什么还需要一个科普项目)

Because most science communication fails families. Academic papers are paywalled and precise to a fault. Popular media chases novelty: "This one trick reverses anxiety!" Parents are left exhausted, either guilted or misled.

Science & Stories operates on quiet luxury principle: less frequency, more care (少而精). Every story must pass three filters: Is it true enough to tell? Is it useful tonight at dinner or bedtime? Is it kind to the child and caregiver? If not all three yes, we don't publish.

We focus where our family lived: emotional regulation (情绪调节), exercise and mood, nature and attention, sleep (睡眠), bilingual development (双语发展), and workplace stress spillover to home. Not comprehensive — curated. The scope matches what we fund in other programs, so writing and practice reinforce.

What we publish — three formats

FORMAT 01 / FIELD NOTE • 日常笔记 • 650 words

One moment, one study, one try

Example: "My daughter said '肚子有蝴蝶' before school — what butterflies mean." Story 150w, research 250w (e.g., Barrett & Simmons on interoceptive prediction), What it doesn't mean 100w (not disorder), Try-at-home 100w: name butterfly color, breathe with it, draw it. Closing line bilingual: "蝴蝶不用赶走，陪它一会，它会飞走 (You don't chase butterflies away, you sit with them)."
Tone: First-person parent, citation inline small.

FORMAT 02 / TRANSLATION • 译介 • 800 words

Landmark paper into plain language

How: Pick one peer-reviewed paper from last 5 years relevant to our pillars. Provide lay summary (通俗总结), methods in one sentence, sample size + population, main effect, limitations, and family relevance. Always include: original DOI link, plain language Chinese abstract (中文摘要 150字).
Example lineup: Stanford Nature vs rumination (Bratman 2015), aerobic exercise & cortisol meta-analysis, affect labeling fMRI Lieberman, Kaplan ART, parental co-regulation.
Guardrail: No p-hacking storytelling. If null result — we still tell it. Null is wisdom too.

FORMAT 03 / GLOSSARY • 词汇表 • visual

Bilingual glossary for feelings & science

We build quiet, beautiful glossary cards: English — 中文 — pinyin — one-line parent explanation — one-line kid metaphor. Example: Amygdala (杏仁核 xìngrénhé) — brain's smoke alarm, not villain. Kid version: "大脑里的小警钟，有时太敏感." Cortisol (皮质醇) — fuel hormone. Kid: "让你有力气的汽油，放太多会累." HRV (心率变异性) — body battery's flexibility. Designed printable, ivory, Cormorant serif Chinese paired with Songti.

EDITORIAL PRINCIPLES • 编辑原则 • Quiet Luxury Science

How we keep trust

1. Cite, don't vibe: Link primary source, include sample N, year. Prefer meta-analyses & preregistered.
2. Effect size + uncertainty: Not "exercise reduces anxiety" but "30 min moderate reduces state anxiety by ~0.4 SD for 2-4h, varies."
3. No miracle language (不制造奇迹): Ban: "cure, fix, hack, reverse." Use: "supports, associates with, may help."
4. Chinese terms when truer: 委屈, 心疼, 恢复, 调节 — keep where English flattens.
5. Child privacy first (儿童隐私): No child identified without consent; stories anonymized, composite where needed.
6. Non-prescriptive: "One try" not "you should." Invitation, not instruction.
7. Open access (开放): All essays Markdown on GitHub, versioned, free to translate.
8. Disclosure: Funding 100% family; no pharma, no supplement sponsors. We list conflicts: we run programs we write about — we state it each time.

From lab to living room (从实验室到客厅)

Our translation pipeline: 1) Alan reads paper + notes methods limitations. 2) Jacqueline writes first-person story tie that grounds it. 3) Pair review against original — did we overstretch causality? 4) Bilingual check — does Chinese term preserve nuance? 5) Family test — read to friend parent, does it make sense at 9pm after bath? 6) Publish on Journal + newsletter, with CC-BY license.

We produce each essay as both web article and printable one-page zine — Seattle Public Library can stock. Quiet design: no stock photos of labs, just paper texture, one botanical drawing, serif type. It should feel like a letter from a friend who read the paper for you.
