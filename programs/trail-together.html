Trail Together
Nature is not a backdrop. It is a protocol.

We hike once a month, 5-8 miles, same community, different Bay Area hills. No work talk first hour. Phones on Do Not Disturb. Family-friendly. Ends with tea and a real check-in. Because belonging is built in repetition.

2nd Saturday • 9:30AMRedwood Regional • Mission Peak • Rancho San AntonioFamily welcomeFree
WHY TRAIL

90 minutes quiets rumination

Stanford Bratman lab: 90-min nature walk reduces activity in subgenual prefrontal cortex — the rumination center — vs. urban walk. Attention Restoration Theory: soft fascination in redwoods restores directed attention depleted by screens.

HOW WE HIKE

No leaderboard, just loop

We do not track PRs. We track: did you show, did you breathe, did you thank someone. Routes loop so no one is left behind. Pacers front and back. First aid, water, headcount clipboard.

BAY AREA ROTATION

East Bay redwoods to South Bay ridges

Redwood Regional Park Stream Trail, Mission Peak via Stanford Ave, Rancho San Antonio, Los Altos Hills — Edith and Deer Hollow, Presidio Ecology Trail, and Golden Gate Park to Lands End. Low smoke season adjustments.

The design: walk, notice, name

Hour 1 — No work talk. We practice attention: notice 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear. Soft fascination. Redwood bark, bay fog, coyote brush. Conversation about non-work life only.

Hour 2 — Optional prompt. One question, no forced sharing: What is one small thing that restored you this week? What is one boundary you kept? What is one feeling you named to your kid?

Summit — Tea + check-in. Ginger tea ritual, one-word close, gratitude for trail crew and Ohlone land stewardship. Leave No Trace, pack out tea bags if needed.

What to bring

Trail shoes, layers for Bay Area microclimates, 1L water, sun protection. We provide tea, cups, first-aid, and cue cards for grounding. Kids in carriers welcome on easy trails (Redwood Stream, Rancho San Antonio). Mission Peak — adults and older kids recommended due to steep heat.

SAFETY • 501(C)(3) PUBLIC CHARITY • EDUCATIONAL ONLY

Not wilderness therapy

We are not a clinical program, not wilderness therapy, not a crisis service. Educational community walks only. Leaders WFA certified, incident plan on file. If you have cardiac, asthma, or severe heat sensitivity, bring clearance and inhaler. We cancel for Air Quality Index >150. We track weather and heat on Mission Peak — start earlier in summer.

For families

Kids 6+ welcome on Redwood and Rancho easy loops. Stroller-friendly only on certain paved sections — email to ask. Teens welcome — Community Service hours available for trail hosts (check school). Dogs: Redwood Regional and Rancho — dogs on leash allowed on some trails, we follow park rules and will note dog-friendly dates.
