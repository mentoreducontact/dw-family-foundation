Trail Together
Monthly hikes that quiet the mind.

Monthly hikes across Bay Area open space. Nature as a practice, not a backdrop. Stanford research guides our format.

Bay AreaFree • Low-costEducational only

Science in plain language

Bratman et al. at Stanford found 90-minute nature walks reduce rumination and activity in brain areas linked to repetitive thought. Attention Restoration Theory suggests natural environments gently hold attention, allowing directed attention to rest. That is why we keep the first hour quiet from work talk.

Format

Second Saturday, 5-8 miles, moderate pace. Family friendly, strollers on paved options. First hour: light conversation or silence. Summit: tea, 3-question check-in, brief grounding exercise. Leave no trace, phone-light.

Locations rotate: Redwood Regional Park, Rancho San Antonio Preserve, Presidio Bay Area Ridge, Mission Peak (occasional), San Bruno Mountain.
