Trail Together
Volunteer-led, foundation-supported.

We don't have capacity to lead every hike — we equip 3-5 volunteer Hike Leaders who do. Pilot Fall 2026: Bay Area trails as free education + practice for contemporary workers (first-gen professionals, immigrant tech workers, young parents juggling work) who lack accessible, culturally competent mental health literacy. Foundation provides trail cards, safety checklists, hormone micro-lessons; volunteers lead 1x/month.

⚠️ Educational Only — Not Medical Advice / 仅教育用途 — Volunteer Role Boundaries

Educational purposes only, not medical advice, not therapy, not a substitute for professional care. We provide preventive mental health literacy education + practical opportunities — 科普教育资源、实践机会、预防性教育 — not treatment, counseling, or crisis intervention. Severe cases need professional treatment. Our Hike Leaders are trained to recognize red flags and refer out, not diagnose or counsel: if someone expresses self-harm, severe functional impairment >2 weeks, psychosis/mania, leaders provide crisis card (988 US / 400-161-9995 CN / 116 123 UK), encourage professional evaluation, contact foundation safety lead, do not counsel. If you are in crisis: 988 (US) / 400-161-9995 (CN) / 116 123 (UK) or ER.

Volunteer-Led, Foundation-Supported — why

Alan & Jacqueline cannot lead every hike — we have a young child and full-time work. For Fall 2026 we pilot 3-5 Hike Leaders (not 10). Small cohort = real 2h training (safety, Leave No Trace, co-regulation basics, waiver & boundaries, red-flag referral, first-aid checklist), real debriefs, real learning. We provide: trail cards for 3 trails, safety checklist, hormone micro-lesson, tea check-in script, educational waiver (not therapy), private volunteer community. Volunteers lead: 1x/month, second Saturday, 6 max, 2-2.5h, phone-light.

Science — education purpose

Bratman et al. Stanford PNAS 2015: 90-min nature walks ↓ rumination and ↓ sgPFC activity vs urban walk, physiological measures no difference — environment effect. For modern workers with mental sub-health lacking access, this science is often paywalled. We translate to free micro-lesson: why ridge→oak canyon gives soft fascination, why cortisol dips 15-25% after 60-70% HR walk.

Who we serve — education equality

Primary pilot — modern workers: First-gen pros who learned calculus not co-regulation, immigrant tech workers facing stigma + hustle, young parents juggling work — educationally underserved in mental health literacy, lacking culturally competent, science-based education + practical opportunities.
Phase 2 — students & littles: Title I students, low-income families 3-8 — same free kits + volunteer model after worker playbook.

Pilot Trails • Fall 2026 • 3-5 volunteer leaders • Volunteer provides cards

Three trails — foundation provides cards, volunteers lead.

All within 90 min of SF, tested for 90-min Stanford dose. Small pilot: 6 people max (not 12), volunteer-led 1x/month, educational only. Foundation provides safety + science micro-lesson for each.

Pilot 01 • South Bay • Moderate • Volunteer card

Rancho San Antonio
Wildcat Loop

4.2 mi / 603 ftLoop • 2–2.5h • 6 max

Why education: Ridge→oak canyon soft fascination — Bratman 90-min ↓ rumination, ↓ sgPFC vs urban, no HR difference. Free micro-lesson for workers who never got manual.

Foundation provides

Trail card, parking full Sat warning, no dogs preserve, 0.8 mi exposed, 1L water, poison oak. Safety checklist, waiver, emergency protocol.

Volunteer leads

08:15 Mora lot, intention card 1 line → 08:30–09:30 silent/low-talk ascent → tea & 3-Q check-in (noticed? let go? carry back?) → grounded descent. Red-flag referral ready, crisis card in pocket.

Hormone micro-lesson — education only

60-70% HRmax → β-endorphin + anandamide ~30 min, cortisol dip 15-25% after nature vs urban. Close with box breathing 4-4-4-4 — education, not treatment.

Pilot 02 • SF • Easy • Volunteer card

Presidio
Bay Area Ridge Trail

2.6 mi / 134 ftPoint-to-point • 1–1.5h • 6 max

Why education: Coastal bluff + eucalyptus soft fascination, low-elevation replica of Stanford 90-min ↓ sgPFC linked to withdrawal. Good for young parents.

Foundation provides

Paved ≥4 ft, gentle ≤5% with 8-12% sections, stroller-friendly all-terrain, roots/steps bypass, coyote dog restrictions, wind layer, Arguello van parking.

Volunteer leads

09:30 Rob Hill, family edition first-gen parents 3+, sensory scavenger 5 textures, adult silent pair-walk 15 min, kids owl eyes, overlook cocoa + 2-min grounding. 6-family cap, LNT. No counseling.

Micro-lesson

Low-intensity <50% VO2 ↑ serotonin via rhythm + light. Co-regulation lowers parental cortisol via social buffering — education, not therapy.

Pilot 03 • East Bay • Forest Bath • Volunteer card

Redwood Regional
West Ridge + French Trail Loop

4.7 mi / 862 ftLoop • 2–2.5h • 6 max

Why education: Redwood fractal greens block urban cues — strongest attention restoration, 90-min nature vs busy 4-lane road ↓ rumination.

Foundation provides

Weekend EBParks fee Apr-Oct Redwood Gate, Canyon Meadow staging restrooms/water, narrow rocky sections boots, leashed dogs ok but pilot no-dog for focus.

Volunteer leads

08:00 start 6 max, 0–15 stream breathing, 15–90 silent forest walk no earbuds, 90–120 Madrone overlook one word + one action for week, optional HRV not medical, litter sweep. Red-flag check, referral list.

Micro-lesson

Terpenes α-pinene ↓ cortisol, moderate climb 600+ ft ↑ BDNF. Oxytocin via social safety supports sgPFC downshift — education, not treatment.

Citation & educational note — not therapy

Bratman et al., 2015, PNAS 112(28):8567-8572 — nature experience reduces rumination and sgPFC activation. 90-min nature walk ↓ rumination vs urban. Effect not due to physiology differences. This is free education + volunteer practice, not treatment. We do NOT replace therapy. Severe cases need professional treatment. For crisis: 988 US / 400-161-9995 CN / 116 123 UK.
