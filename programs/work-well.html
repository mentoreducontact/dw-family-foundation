Work Well
Burnout is not a personality flaw. It is a design flaw.

For Bay Area workers 25-45 — engineers, PMs, designers, nurses, teachers — who are exhausted, cynical, and wondering if they are just bad at adult jobs. WHO defines burnout as exhaustion + cynicism + inefficacy from chronic unmanaged stress. We teach boundaries, recovery, and manager skills without worshipping hustle.

Workshops 60-90 minManagers + ICsBay Area companies • Nonprofit rateEducational only
WHO

Tech teams + human-heavy roles

Tech workers in South Bay and San Francisco, plus teachers and caregivers. High autonomy + high demand + low recovery = risk. We tailor for remote, hybrid, and on-call.

WHAT

Boundaries, recovery, repair

Evidence-based: boundary scripts, micro-recovery between meetings, manager 1:1s that ask about load not just output, no-meeting mornings. No icebreakers that embarrass.

OUTCOME

Not happiness theater

We measure perceived stress short scale, sleep hours, intent to stay, and meeting load. Not NPS happiness theater. Real work: fewer after-hours pings, protected lunch walks in Presidio or along Embarcadero.

Module 1 — Know the line: stress vs burnout vs depression

Stress: Too much, but coping — can recover with weekend, sleep. Burnout: Chronic, depleted, cynical, low efficacy — even after rest, dread returns. Depression: Persists across domains, anhedonia, hopelessness, needs clinical evaluation. We teach when to self-care, when to manager-action, when to refer. Educational only, with 988 and EAP list.

Module 2 — Boundary scripts that actually work in Bay Area tech

“I can take that on Thursday if we deprioritize X, or Friday if Y stays priority — which would you prefer?” Not “No.” Choice architecture. Also: calendar blocks for lunch walks, notifications off after 7pm, on-call handoff ritual. Managers model first.

Module 3 — Manager training 45 min

Ask workload in numbers, not “how are you holding?” — “On a 1-10, how overloaded this sprint?” Protect 10% buffer. Run blameless retros that include human impact. Check your own burnout — exhausted managers create exhausted teams.

WHAT MANAGERS CAN DO TOMORROW
  • Cancel one recurring meeting permanently and note why
  • Declare no-meeting Wednesday morning for focus and walk
  • Add “recovery” to retro — not just velocity
  • Share your own boundary: “I do not check Slack after 8pm”
