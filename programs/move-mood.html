Move & Mood Lab
Movement as regulation, not punishment.

Weekly runs and walks in Golden Gate Park and Presidio. We teach simple biology so habits stick. No personal records, no shame.

Bay AreaFree • Low-costEducational only

Why movement matters for mood

Burnout is not just a mindset. It shows up as nervous system activation: shallow breathing, tense shoulders, cortisol that stays high. Easy to moderate movement helps complete the stress cycle. Research: a single 30-minute bout at 60-70% max heart rate lowers cortisol 15-25% in the following hour, improves mood for 2-4 hours, and raises heart rate variability, a marker of recovery. Done weekly, it improves sleep onset, steady energy, and sense of control.

How a session works

Check-in. Three quick ratings: sleep, energy, stress 1-5. Optional HRV from your watch.
Breath + mobility. Diaphragmatic breathing and gentle mobility for ankles and hips.
Community miles. Three groups: Stroll, Steady, Flow. Loops, no one left behind.
Strength + close. Bodyweight basics + box breathing to finish. One-word close.
