Move & Mood Lab
A lab where sweat becomes data, and data becomes calm.

Exercise is not punishment. It is regulation. For adults who live in laptops and carry tension in shoulders, we run — slowly, together, with breath as the metric that matters. No PRs. No shame. Bay Area fog is our air conditioning.

Weekly • Saturdays 8:30AMFree • No membershipGolden Gate Park + PresidioAll paces • Walk-to-run
WHO

Workers, parents, caregivers

Adults who have not moved in months, who feel wired but tired. Parents who only get time after drop-off. No athletic history required. If you can walk for 20 minutes, you belong. Range 26 to 68.

WHAT

Weekly runs, strength + breathwork

60-minute sessions: 10 min check-in + breathwork, 35 min community run or walk intervals, 10 min bodyweight strength, 5 min box breathing cool-down. Coaches track energy, not pace.

SCIENCE

Why 30 minutes changes everything

Moderate exercise reduces cortisol 15-25% within an hour post-exercise. Endorphins lift mood, serotonin steadies rumination, BDNF — fertilizer for neurons — rises with aerobic work, supporting learning and stress resilience.

Why movement is mood medicine

Burnout is not a mindset problem. It is a nervous system state: sympathetic dominance, shallow breathing, cortisol that never lands. The lab treats movement as a physiological reset, not a productivity hack.

Research we teach every cohort: a single 30-minute bout of moderate-intensity exercise — 60-70% HRmax, able to speak in short sentences — lowers salivary cortisol, improves affect for 2-4 hours, and increases HRV, a proxy for parasympathetic recovery. Repeated weekly for 8 weeks, it remodels baseline: better sleep latency, lower anxiety scores, higher self-efficacy.

We do not worship intensity. We worship consistency. The neurochemistry stack: endorphin for immediate calm, serotonin for stable mood, dopamine for motivation to return, and BDNF for long-term plasticity. That is why we pair movement with breathwork — to teach your body what calm feels like, so you can find it again on Tuesday at 3pm when email spikes.

How a Saturday works — Bay Area edition

08:30 — Arrival & state check. 3-question check: sleep 1-5, energy 1-5, stress 1-5. Optional HRV via watch. We teach reading it as information, not judgment. Meet at Golden Gate Park — Conservatory Grove or Presidio — Lovers Lane.

08:40 — Breath + mobility. Diaphragmatic breathing 4-6 pattern, ankle and hip mobility. Nasal breathing lowers threat response.

08:50 — Community miles. Three pace groups: Stroll 13-15 min/mile, Steady 10-12, Flow 8-10. Walk-run allowed. Looped routes, no one left behind. Fog, eucalyptus, and occasional coyote.

09:25 — Strength + breathwork. 2 rounds bodyweight squats, incline push-ups, deadbug, glute bridge. Between: box breathing 4-4-4-4.

09:35 — Close. One-word close, water, optional coffee in the Richmond or Presidio.

Science, simply told

Cortisol is not evil — it is fuel. Chronic elevation is the problem. Moderate aerobic work burns excess cortisol through metabolism and negative feedback to HPA axis. Endorphins bind to opioid receptors — natural ease after 20+ min. Serotonin synthesis benefits from movement and bright light — both outdoors. BDNF rises most after 30+ min at moderate effort, supporting hippocampal health — the brain area that shrinks under chronic stress.

Principles: implementation intention (if Saturday 8:30, then Golden Gate Park), social accountability without comparison, and attention restoration from green and ocean air.

This is not therapy. It is adjacent and accessible. For clinical mood concerns, we refer to partner clinicians and crisis resources. Movement supports, not replaces, professional care.
