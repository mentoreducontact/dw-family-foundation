Move & Mood Lab
A lab where sweat becomes data, and data becomes calm.

Exercise is not punishment. It is regulation (调节). For burnt-out adults who live in Slack and carry tension in their shoulders, we run — slowly, together, with breath as the metric that matters. No PRs. No shame.

Weekly • Saturdays 8:30AMFree • No membershipHRV tracking optional • 心率变异性All paces • Walk-to-run
WHO • 为谁

Burnt-out adults, caregivers, tech workers

Adults (成年人) who haven't moved in months, who feel wired but tired. Parents who only get time after drop-off. No athletic history required. If you can walk for 20 minutes, you belong. We are intentionally intergenerational — 26 to 68 is our current range.

WHAT • 做什么

Weekly runs, strength + breathwork

60-minute sessions: 10 min check-in + breathwork (呼吸练习), 35 min community run / walk intervals, 10 min bodyweight strength (深蹲, 俯卧撑, 平板), 5 min box breathing cool-down. Coaches track perceived energy, not pace. You leave regulated, not wrecked.

SCIENCE • 科学依据

Why 30 minutes changes everything

Moderate exercise reduces cortisol (皮质醇) 15-25% within 30-60 min post-exercise. Endorphins (内啡肽) lift pain threshold and mood. Serotonin (血清素) steadies rumination. BDNF (脑源性神经营养因子) — fertilizer for your neurons — rises with aerobic work, supporting learning and stress resilience.

Why movement is mood medicine (运动即良药)

Burnout (倦怠) is not a mindset problem. It is a nervous system state: sympathetic dominance, shallow breathing, cortisol that never lands. The lab treats movement as a physiological reset, not a productivity hack.

Research we teach every cohort: a single 30-minute bout of moderate-intensity exercise — 60-70% HRmax, able to speak in short sentences — lowers salivary cortisol by 15-25% in the hour after, improves affect for 2-4 hours, and increases HRV (心率变异性), a proxy for parasympathetic recovery. Repeated weekly for 8 weeks, it remodels baseline: better sleep latency, lower anxiety scores, higher self-efficacy.

We do not worship intensity. We worship consistency (一致性). The neurochemistry stack we reference: endorphin for immediate calm, serotonin for stable mood, dopamine for motivation to return, and BDNF for long-term brain plasticity. That is why we pair movement with breathwork — to teach your body what calm feels like, so you can find it again on Tuesday at 3pm when email spikes.

How a Saturday works

08:30 — Arrival & state check (状态签到). 3-question check: sleep 1-5, energy 1-5, stress 1-5. No sharing required. Optional HRV via Oura / Whoop / Apple Watch. We teach you to read it as information, not judgment.

08:40 — Breath + mobility. Diaphragmatic breathing (腹式呼吸) 4-6 pattern, ankle / hip mobility. We explain why nasal breathing lowers threat response.

08:50 — Community miles. Three pace groups: Stroll (散步组) 13-15 min/mile, Steady (稳定组) 10-12, Flow (流动组) 8-10. Walk-run allowed. No one left behind — looped routes in Seward Park. Coaches run at the back.

09:25 — Strength + breathwork. 2 rounds: bodyweight squats, incline push-ups, deadbug, glute bridge. Between sets: box breathing (箱式呼吸) 4-4-4-4. We end supine, 2 minutes eyes closed.

09:35 — Close. One-word close, water, optional coffee nearby. Data sync if you tracked.

What to bring (带什么)

Keep it quiet luxury: less is more. Running shoes you have (no need for carbon plates), breathable layers (Seattle morning = 50-58°F), water bottle, optional HR tracker. We provide: cue cards for breathing, first-aid, liability waiver you sign once. We do not provide: competition, timing chips, motivational shouting. If you are postpartum, injured, or managing a condition, bring clearance — we will modify.

For HRV tracking (心率变异性追踪), we teach a simple morning routine: wake, sit, 2-min measure before coffee. Trend over 7 days matters more than one day. Higher resting HRV = more capacity. We use it to teach permission to go slow.

Science, simply told (把科学讲成人话)

We translate peer-reviewed work into human language (科普). Cortisol is not evil — it is fuel. Chronic elevation is the problem. Moderate aerobic work burns excess cortisol through metabolism and negative feedback to HPA axis. Endorphins bind to opioid receptors — natural analgesia after 20+ min. Serotonin synthesis requires movement and bright light — both present outdoors. BDNF rises most after 30+ min at 60%+ effort, supporting hippocampal health — the brain area that shrinks under chronic stress.

Our principles borrowed from behavioral science: implementation intention (if Saturday 8:30, then Seward Park), social accountability without comparison (we run with not against), and attention restoration from green + water — Seward's old-growth cedars do part of the coaching.

This is not therapy. It is adjacent and accessible. For clinical mood concerns, we refer to partner clinicians and crisis resources. Movement supports, not replaces, professional care (运动是辅助，不是替代治疗).
