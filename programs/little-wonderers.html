Little Wonderers
Emotional literacy is a skill, not a trait.

For young children 3-8, feelings are big and words are new. Little Wonderers teaches emotional literacy through play, stories, games, and parent co-regulation — no screens, no apps. Naming is taming. When a child can say “I am frustrated,” they are less likely to hit.

Ages 3-8 • Parent + childPlay • Stories • Co-regulationBay Area libraries & parksFree printable kit
WHO

Kids 3-8 + caring adults

Not kids alone. We teach through the adult who co-regulates. Parents, grandparents, nannies, preschool teachers in the Bay Area. Small groups 6-10 families, libraries in Berkeley, Oakland, San Mateo, and parks.

WHAT

Story pause-and-name, games, body cues

We read a picture book, pause: What is the character feeling? Where in body? What helps? Then practice: breath like blowing bubbles, shake like a dog, squeeze like a lemon. Take-home cards for fridge.

WHY EARLY

Skills before stigma

Child Mind Institute: emotional literacy at 3-8 predicts later academic and social outcomes better than IQ. Labeling emotions reduces amygdala reactivity. Co-regulation from a calm adult wires self-regulation over time. No diagnosis needed.

How a 45-minute session works

0-5 min — Arrival & body check. Welcome song, stretch tall like redwoods, small like acorns. Where is your energy? High like Mission Peak or cozy like fog?

5-20 min — Story & pause. Picture book about sadness, anger, excitement, worry. Pause: What do you see in face? What might they do next? Adults model: “I feel ___ when ___, and I need ___.”

20-35 min — Play to practice. Four stations: feelings matching cards, body map coloring, calm corner with pillows and pinwheels for breathing, and “what helps” jar. No winners.

35-45 min — Co-regulation script for home. Parents get 3-sentence script: Name, Validate, Offer (“You are frustrated. It is hard to wait. Do you want to squeeze or breathe?”) Plus fridge card: 5 feelings, where in body, 3 helps.

What we provide free

Printable feelings cards (12 emotions), body sensations sheet, calm corner poster, parent script one-pager. All designed in our cream and forest palette with instrument sans — calm, not cartoon circus. Print on ivory paper. Laminate if you like.

FOR PARENTS • CO-REGULATION

You do not have to be calm perfectly

Co-regulation does not mean never feeling angry. It means letting your child see you pause, name, and choose. “I am feeling overwhelmed. I am going to take 3 breaths, then we will figure it out.” That is the lesson. Bay Area parents juggle a lot — commute, mortgage, school lotteries. We keep tools under 2 minutes, usable at Rancho San Antonio parking lot.

Where

Berkeley Public Library, Oakland parks, San Mateo County libraries, and community rooms in San Francisco — Presidio and Golden Gate Park picnic areas weather permitting. By RSVP, free. Donations optional. No child data collected. Programs go through adults only.
