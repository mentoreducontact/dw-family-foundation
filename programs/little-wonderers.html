Program 04 / Early Childhood • 小小好奇家 • Ages 3-8 • Story-based

Little Wonderers
Feelings have names. Stories help us find them.

Emotional literacy (情绪素养) starts before reading. For kids 3-8 (三至八岁), we use storytelling, pause, and naming — the core of Daily Wonder podcast (小小好奇家播客) — to build a vocabulary for inner weather. Not discipline tools. Wonder tools.

Bilingual EN/中文 • 双语For families & preschoolsRooted in Daily Wonder podcast免费资源
WHO • 为谁

Kids 3-8, parents, early educators

Children learning to identify and express big feelings (大大的情绪), parents who want scripts not lectures, preschool teachers (幼儿园老师) seeking quiet, low-stim circle time that doesn't require screens. Especially first-gen bilingual families navigating two feeling-cultures.

WHAT • 做什么

Story pause-and-name + feelings chart

Each Daily Wonder episode becomes a 10-min classroom ritual: listen, pause at feeling peak, name it with chart, breathe, continue. Tools: feelings chart (情绪表), story pause card (故事暂停卡), body scan for kids (身体扫描), family dinner prompt. All print-ready, ivory-paper beautiful.

SCIENCE • 科学

Naming calms the brain

Affect labeling (情绪命名, Lieberman et al.) — putting words to feelings reduces amygdala reactivity. Narrative transportation supports empathy. Co-listening builds attachment (依恋). Early emotional literacy predicts later self-regulation and academic readiness (自我调节).

Why stories work before reason does (为什么故事比道理管用)

A child's prefrontal cortex is under construction until ~25. Logic lectures bounce off. Stories don't — they travel via mirror neurons. When a Daily Wonder character says "我的胸口像有一块石头" (my chest feels like a rock), a 4-year-old recognizes stone-in-chest without needing to define anxiety (焦虑). We teach that recognition is the victory.

Little Wonderers is intentionally slow media (慢媒体). One story per week, same story 2-3 times. Repetition is not boredom for this age — it's mastery. Our pause-and-name method: the adult pauses the audio at a feeling moment, shows the feelings chart, asks "你觉得他现在是什么感觉? 你在哪里感觉到? (What feeling? Where in your body?)" No correct answer. The goal is interoception + vocabulary.

Tie to Daily Wonder podcast (与播客联动)

Daily Wonder is our family podcast — short, wonder-filled stories in English and Mandarin about science, emotions, and families that wonder together. Little Wonderers takes each episode and adds three layers:

1) Pre-listen ritual (听之前): 30-sec breathing with stuffed animal belly breath (毛绒玩具肚子呼吸) — child lies toy on belly, watches it rise. We call it "让小熊睡觉" / "rocking bear to sleep." Sets parasympathetic tone.

2) Pause points (暂停点): Timestamped in show notes. e.g., Episode "Why does thunder sound angry? (为什么打雷听起来很生气?)" — pause at 3:12 when protagonist hides under blanket. Ask: "如果害怕有颜色，是什么颜色 (If fear had a color)?"

3) After-story action (听之后): 5-min drawing: weather map of feelings. Today internal weather? 晴天/多云/下雨/打雷. Or move the feeling out: shake, stomp, hug. We provide printable worksheet: My Inner Weather Report (我的内心天气预报).

This is not screen time replacement. It is co-listening time (共同收听) — parent + child + story. Research: co-experiencing increases emotional coaching quality versus passive media. Ten minutes, presence over production value.

Core tools — open source, beautiful

TOOL 01 • 情绪表

Feelings Chart (24 emotions)

Not happy/sad binary. We show 24 faces, bilingual, organized by energy + pleasantness (core affect). High energy unpleasant: 生气的, 紧张, frustrated. Low energy unpleasant: 难过, lonely, tired. Kids point. Validated Chinese terms reviewed by bilingual clinicians. Print A3 for wall, A5 for pocket. Quiet palette: ivory, clay, moss.
设计理念: 给情绪一个家，不是标签，是入住.

TOOL 02 • 故事暂停卡

Story Pause-and-Name Card

A bookmark for adults: on one side, three prompts: Pause (暂停), Name (命名), Stay (陪伴). On other, 6 sentence starters: "听起来你感觉..." "你的身体告诉你什么?" "想要一个抱抱还是空间?" Includes QR to episode. Laminated quiet luxury — feels good in hand. We distribute free to Seattle Public Library branches (西雅图公共图书馆).

TOOL 03 • 身体扫描 for kids

Body Scan — "Where do you feel it?"

Kids don't say "I'm anxious." They say "肚子疼." We teach body map: head (思绪多), chest (心跳快), belly (蝴蝶), hands (想要打). 2-min script: "从头到脚，像扫描仪" — with chime. Builds interoception (内感受), predictor of regulation.
Use before sleep, after tantrum, not during peak storm — wait for 7-min post.

TOOL 04 • 家庭晚餐问题

Family Dinner Prompts (Wonder Table)

Replacement for "How was school?" — 52 cards: "今天哪个时刻你感到好奇? 好奇时身体哪里?" "你帮情绪起了什么名字?" Bilingual. One per dinner. Builds emotional culture at home (家庭情绪文化), not just at school. Parents report kids start initiating.

How preschools use it (幼儿园怎么用)

Circle time (10 min): Play 4-min excerpt, pause, feelings chart, 2 kids share, close with belly breath. Teacher does not fix feelings — witnesses them. Script: "谢谢你告诉我们，那个感觉一定很大 (Thank you for telling us, that feeling sounds big)."

Transition support: Many meltdowns happen at transitions (过渡). We use same story's closing song as transition cue — auditory anchor (声音锚点). Predictability lowers cortisol for toddlers.

Parent circle: Monthly 30-min caregiver evening (家长圈): practice pause-and-name with own childhood story — breaks intergenerational pattern where feelings were silenced (不许哭). Facilitated, not taught top-down.

A NOTE ON BILINGUAL FEELINGS • 双语情绪笔记

Some feelings don't translate cleanly — and that's a gift

委屈 (wěiqu) has no single English word — feeling wronged + sadness + not able to explain. 心疼 (xīnténg) is "heart hurts for you" — empathic ache. We keep them. When English lacks a word, we borrow. This teaches kids that language expands feeling, not limits it. Our chart includes 委屈, 心疼, 紧张 with pinyin + story context, not dictionary definition.
