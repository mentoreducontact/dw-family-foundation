Privacy is a promise, not a policy.

We are a small, family-run foundation that helps people move from psychological sub-health to belonging. We collect as little as possible, keep it only as long as we need it, and never trade on your trust. This policy explains what we collect, why, and how you remain in control.

01 — Introduction

Who we are and why this matters

DW Family Foundation (“DWFF”, “we”, “us”) is a family foundation based in Seattle, Washington, focused on psychological sub-health education through movement, nature, and storytelling. Our website is informational and educational only.

We operate on the principle of data minimalism. If we don’t need it to send a receipt, answer your note, or understand whether a page helped, we don’t collect it. If we do collect it, we treat it as a loan — not an asset.

This Privacy Policy covers information collected through our website, donation forms, newsletter sign-ups, and direct correspondence with us at mentoredu.contact@gmail.com.

02 — What we collect

Only what we need to serve you

We group collection into six quiet categories. We do not build profiles, we do not infer sensitive characteristics, and we do not purchase data from brokers.

Contact information

Name, email address, and any message you choose to send when you contact us, subscribe for updates, volunteer, or partner. We use this only to reply and to keep the thread of conversation.

Donation information

For donors: name, email, mailing address (for tax receipts when required), donation amount, date, frequency, campaign attribution, and method. We do not store full payment card numbers, bank accounts, or CVV codes. Those are handled directly by our PCI-DSS compliant processors.

Technical & IP data

Standard web logs: IP address (truncated where possible), browser type, operating system, referrer, pages viewed, and timestamps. This helps us debug and understand which essays are useful. We do not fingerprint.

Cookies & similar

Today we use only essential, functional cookies required for the site to load. No advertising cookies. See Cookies section for our future privacy-friendly analytics plan.

GitHub Pages logs

Our site is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may generate server logs including IP addresses and request metadata as part of providing hosting, CDN, and security. This processing is governed by GitHub’s own privacy statement and Data Protection Agreement.

Stripe & payment processors

When you donate via Stripe (or PayPal when offered), the processor collects payment details, device data for fraud prevention, and confirmation of payment. We receive only a token, last four digits, expiry month/year, confirmation status, and your contact details — never the full PAN. Stripe’s use of data is governed by Stripe’s privacy policy.

03 — How we use

Stewardship, not marketing

We use information solely to:

  • Process donations, issue tax receipts, and maintain legally required nonprofit records;
  • Respond to inquiries, coordinate hikes, runs, classroom kits, and volunteer work;
  • Send the occasional, low-volume update you asked for — no weekly blasts, no drip sequence;
  • Maintain the security, integrity, and performance of our site;
  • Understand, in aggregate and anonymously, which topics (movement science, nature, emotional literacy) resonate, so we can write better;
  • Comply with law, accounting, and our charitable purpose.

We do not use your information for automated decision-making, scoring, or unrelated secondary purposes.

04 — Sharing — we do not sell

We share only to operate

Trust is not transferable. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

We share personal information only with:

  • Processors who help us operate: GitHub (hosting), Google Workspace / Gmail for correspondence at mentoredu.contact@gmail.com, Stripe and PayPal for payments, and in the future Plausible Analytics (EU-hosted, no cookies). Each processor acts under contract and only on our instructions.
  • Legal compliance: when required by law, valid legal process, to protect our rights, or to meet nonprofit accounting and 990 obligations.
  • With your explicit consent: for example, if you ask us to introduce you to a school or trail partner.

We never rent lists. We never add you to another organization’s list. If you donate offline by check or wire, the same rule applies.

05 — Donor data promise

Our binding commitment to donors

Donor Promise — in our own words

We will not sell, share or trade our donors’ names or personal information with any other entity, nor send mailings to our donors on behalf of other organizations.

This sentence is not marketing language. It governs all donor data we receive online and offline, via electronic, written, or oral communications.

  • Offline cash/check/wire gifts are included.
  • Recurring and one-time gifts are included.
  • Anonymous gifts remain anonymous — we do not attempt to re-identify.
  • We do not use donor information for third-party prospecting, wealth screening, or list appends.

See our Donor Privacy page for the full lifecycle and what we store versus what we never store.

06 — Cookies

Essentials today, Plausible tomorrow

Currently our site does not set non-essential tracking cookies. We use only what is strictly necessary for pages to load over CDN and retain no advertising identifiers.

We plan to add Plausible Analytics — a privacy-friendly, EU-hosted analytics tool that does not use cookies, does not collect IP in full, does not create cross-site profiles, and is GDPR compliant without consent banners. When enabled, Plausible will help us count visits to essays like What is mental sub-health? without identifying you. We will update this policy when that happens, but our posture remains no advertising, no tracking.

You may disable or delete cookies in your browser at any time. The site will continue to work.

Read our full Cookie Policy.

07 — GitHub Pages

Where our site lives

This site is built as static HTML and hosted on GitHub Pages, operated by GitHub, Inc. GitHub provides the edge network, TLS encryption, and resilience that lets a tiny family foundation have a fast, reliable site without running our own servers.

As part of that service, GitHub may collect and process technical logs (including IP addresses) for security, compliance, and availability. We do not control GitHub’s infrastructure logs, but we choose this host because of its transparency, limited retention, and public DPA. GitHub’s own privacy notice governs that processing.

08 — Security

Reasonable care for a small foundation

We use TLS everywhere, restrict administrative access, keep our code static and auditable, and rely on reputable processors (Stripe, GitHub) who maintain SOC 2 and PCI-DSS controls. No system is perfect. We retain no full payment credentials on our servers — this is intentional architecture to reduce risk.

If we learn of a security incident that affects your personal information, we will notify you by email if feasible and as required by law.

09 — Retention

Keep short, keep necessary

We keep contact correspondence for up to 24 months after last activity, unless you ask us to delete earlier. Donation records are kept for seven years to satisfy IRS nonprofit record-keeping, state charity accounting, and audit requirements. Technical logs from our own observation (if any) are retained for 12 months and then aggregated or deleted. GitHub and Stripe have their own retention windows under their policies.

10 — Children’s Privacy — COPPA

We do not knowingly collect children’s data

Our site is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. In compliance with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), if we learn we have inadvertently collected personal information from a child under 13, we will delete it promptly.

Our Little Wonderers program serves ages 3-8 through parents and educators, not directly to children. We do not run child accounts, do not track child behavior, do not collect names or emails of children, and all printable feelings wheels and story guides are available without providing any child’s information. Parents participate, children benefit — that is the boundary.

11 — Your Rights

Even though we are exempt, we honor your rights

As a nonprofit charitable organization, we are currently exempt from the CCPA/CPRA and similar state privacy laws. We choose to honor the spirit of those laws anyway, because it is best practice.

  • Access & correction: You may request what personal information we hold about you and ask us to correct inaccuracies.
  • Deletion: You may request deletion, subject to legal retention for IRS and nonprofit accounting. Payment data held by Stripe must be deleted via Stripe processes, which we will facilitate.
  • Opt-out: You may opt out at any time from non-essential communications, from any future sale or sharing (even though we do not sell or share), and from any future analytics cookies. See Do Not Sell or Share.
  • Portability: Where feasible, we will provide your data in a portable format.

We respond to verifiable requests within 45 days. If we need more time, we will tell you why. We will not discriminate against you for exercising privacy rights.

13 — Changes

When this policy changes

If we add Plausible Analytics, change processors, or materially change retention, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top and, where appropriate, provide additional notice via our site footer or email if you subscribed. Continued use after changes means you accept the updated policy. For material changes that involve new data sharing, we will seek affirmative consent where required.

This policy is educational and does not constitute legal advice. Drafted with reference to Association of Fundraising Professionals Donor Bill of Rights, CCPA, and nonprofit best practices.
