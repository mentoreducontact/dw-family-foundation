Cookie Policy — just enough crumbs.

We use minimal cookies: essential for GitHub Pages to serve you securely, fraud prevention for Stripe donations, and future privacy-first analytics (Plausible) that uses no cross-site cookies. No ad pixels. No retargeting.

No advertising cookiesNo Meta pixel • No Google AdsGPC honored

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored by your browser. Some are “first-party” set by our domain (github.io), some “third-party” set by Stripe when you donate. This policy explains each.

Cookies we do NOT use

  • Advertising: No Facebook Pixel (fbc, fbp), no Google Ads (gcl_au, _gcl), no TikTok, no LinkedIn Insight.
  • Cross-site tracking: No sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • Social widgets that track: We do not embed YouTube / social feeds that set cookies before click.

Analytics plan — Plausible, privacy-first

We plan to add Plausible analytics (self-hosted or via proxy), which is GDPR-friendly: no cookies, no persistent identifiers, no cross-site tracking, IP anonymized, data aggregated, hosted EU/US, no consent required under PECR for anonymized analytics. It measures total page views (e.g., which article helps most) — not who you are. If we add it, we will update this page and no banner will be required under strict consent models, but we will still provide opt-out: append ?no-stats or set DNT.

Until then, we rely on GitHub traffic insights (aggregate, per GitHub privacy).

How to control cookies

Browser settings: You can block or delete cookies in your browser settings. How to: Chrome > Settings > Privacy > Third-party cookies; Safari > Settings > Privacy > Block all cookies; Firefox > Settings > Privacy > Cookies. Blocking may cause Stripe checkout to fail (fraud check needs temporary cookie) — allow stripe.com if needed.

Global Privacy Control: We honor GPC signal as Do Not Sell/Share — see Do Not Sell.

Do Not Track: DNT signal also honored as opt-out for analytics if we enable it.
