About • Bay Area Family Foundation

We are a Bay Area family building a quiet layer of support before crisis.

We are Alan and Jacqueline — parents, neighbors, and long-time Bay Area residents. We started DW Family Foundation as a 501(c)(3) private foundation because we kept seeing the same gap: kids can name dinosaurs but not their anger, adults can ship products but not name burnout, students ace formulas but freeze before exams. Most people are not in crisis, but not quite steady either. That everyday middle is where we work.

Founders

Alan & Jacqueline

Alan works in technology and knows what sustained pressure feels like. Jacqueline focuses on family, community, and making gatherings actually warm. We are parents of a young child who taught us that emotional skills at 4 are more useful than coping at 34.

Based in the Bay Area. Small circle, no plans for rapid scale.

What we borrowed

Standing on proven shoulders

  • Active Minds: peer support works better than top-down advice.
  • The Jed Foundation: life transitions are high-risk and need toolkits.
  • Mind: clear science beats hype.
  • Child Mind Institute: emotional skills are teachable.
  • Bring Change to Mind: normal stories reduce stigma.

The gap we chose

The middle zone is not a diagnosis. It is a signal. WHO data suggests about half of people will face a mental health condition in their lifetime, but years before that many live in a stretched state: trouble falling asleep, afternoon crashes, irritability, endless scrolling, Sunday scaries, looping thoughts. US surveys show over 70% report weekly stress symptoms (APA). Clinics are full. Prevention is mostly empty. We fill the quiet, local part.

We are educational only. We do not provide therapy, diagnosis, or crisis services.

Three principles

01

Local and walkable

We run things you can walk to: a morning session in Golden Gate Park, a trail day in the East Bay hills, a calm kit for a nearby classroom. Record what we did. Share it openly.

02

Science, clearly told

Every program points to a mechanism: cortisol shifts with easy pace, mood lifts with rhythmic movement, attention restores in green space. No fads, no crystals, no supplements.

03

Together, not alone

We do not build an app to fix loneliness. We meet on the same trail on the second Saturday. Belonging comes from repetition and being known.

What we do, week by week

Weekdays
Move & Mood Lab — Morning community movement + breathwork in San Francisco. Free. All paces.
2nd Saturday
Trail Together — Monthly Bay Area hikes, 5-8 miles, light phone use, family friendly. Tea and check-in at the top.
School term
Classroom Calm — Simple toolkits for local schools: breathing cards, grounding posters, movement timers.
Family
Little Wonderers — Emotional skills for ages 3-8 through play and story. For parents and caregivers together.

What we do not do

  • We do not provide therapy, diagnosis, or crisis care. For urgent help in the US, call or text 988.
  • We do not sell supplements, courses, or expensive retreats.
  • We do not run ads or sell data.
