About • Family + Science • 心理亚健康

We started with a kids podcast.
Now we’re building the layer underneath: mental sub-health.

DW = Daily Wonder, Alan and Jacqueline’s kids podcast. After 100+ nights putting a small person to sleep with stories, we kept seeing the same gap: kids can name dinosaurs but not their own anger. Adults can ship code but not name burnout. Students can memorize formulas but freeze before exams. That gray zone — not sick, not fine — is called 心理亚健康, psychological sub-health. That’s where we work.

Why us

Alan & Jacqueline

Alan — tech worker who knows tech burnout. Jacqueline — mother, operator, community builder. One kid who taught us that emotional literacy at 4 is better than therapy at 34.

Seattle-based, small donor circle, no plans to scale fast.

What we borrowed

Standing on proven shoulders

  • Active Minds: peer-to-peer beats top-down. Students listen to students.
  • The Jed Foundation: transitions (middle school → high school → first job) are highest risk, need toolkits.
  • Mind UK: translate science into human language. No hype.
  • Child Mind Institute: kids emotions are skills, not character.
  • Bring Change 2 Mind: stigma dies when stories are normal.

The problem we chose

Psychological sub-health is not a diagnosis. It’s a signal. WHO says 1 in 2 people will face a mental health condition in lifetime, but long before that — 2-5 years — they live in sub-health: poor sleep (入睡困难), daytime fog, irritability, doomscrolling, Sunday scaries, 3pm crash, rumination (脑内小剧场). In China, 2023 CDC survey: 34.6% of adults report sub-health mood; US APA: 73% report stress symptoms weekly.

Clinics are full, prevention is empty. We fill the empty part.

Three principles

01

Small is the feature

We run walkable things: a run club in one park, a hike once a month, a calm kit in three classrooms. Publish receipts. If it works, others copy.

02

Science, not superstition

Every program links to mechanism: cortisol ↓ with Zone 2, endorphin + anandamide = runner’s high, serotonin from rhythmic movement, BDNF for memory, attention restoration from green. No crystals.

03

Community-rooted

We don’t build an app to solve loneliness. We invite people to same trail second Saturday. Belonging is repetitive, boring, and local.

What we actually do, week by week

Mondays
Move & Mood Lab — 6:30am Green Lake community run + breathwork (free). HRV check, 30 min talk on hormone science.
2nd Saturday
Trail Together — monthly hike, 5-8 miles, no-work-talk hour, family-friendly, kids welcome. Ends with tea + feelings check.
Classroom
Classroom Calm — calm kits to 3 pilot schools: box breathing cards, 5-4-3-2-1 posters, movement break timers.
Podcast
Little Wonderers — Daily Wonder kids episodes labeled “emotional literacy” + printable feelings wheel (Chinese/English).

What we don’t do

  • We don’t provide therapy, diagnosis, or crisis service. We are educational only. If you need help now: 988 in US, 400-161-9995 in China, or text.
  • We don’t sell supplements, courses, or retreats.
  • We don’t scale with ads.
