We are a Bay Area family foundation
for the part before crisis.

DW Family Foundation was started by a small family in the Bay Area who kept seeing the same pattern: adults who can ship products but cannot name burnout, students who can ace tests but freeze before exams, and 4-year-olds who can name dinosaurs but not frustration. That gray zone — not sick, not fine — is where prevention lives. That is where we work.

Why us

Bay Area Family

We are parents, community members, and longtime Bay Area residents. We know tech work, school pressure, and how expensive and hard it is to find support early. DW stands for our family initials — a promise to keep this small, careful, and accountable.

What we borrowed

Standing on proven shoulders

  • Active Minds: peer-to-peer beats top-down. Students listen to students.
  • The Jed Foundation: transitions are highest risk — need toolkits.
  • Mind UK: translate science into human language. No hype.
  • Child Mind Institute: early emotional skills are skills, not character.
  • Bring Change 2 Mind: stigma dies when stories are normal.

The problem we chose

Everyday mental sub-health is not a diagnosis. It is a signal. WHO notes 1 in 2 people will face a mental health condition in a lifetime, but years before that they live in sub-health: poor sleep, daytime fog, irritability, doomscrolling, Sunday scaries, afternoon crash, rumination. APA 2023: 73% of US adults report weekly stress symptoms. Clinics are full. Prevention is empty. We fill the empty part with practices you can do before you need a referral.

We are educational only. Not medical, not therapy, not crisis. If you need immediate help, call or text 988 in the US.

Three principles

01

Small is the feature

We run walkable things: a run club in one park, a hike once a month on East Bay trails, a calm kit in three classrooms. Publish receipts. If it works, others copy.

02

Science, not superstition

Every program links to mechanism: cortisol shifts with Zone 2 movement, endorphin and anandamide support runner's high, serotonin from rhythmic movement, BDNF for memory, attention restoration from green. No crystals.

03

Community-rooted

We do not build an app to solve loneliness. We invite people to the same trail second Saturday. Belonging is repetitive, local, and in person. Presidio, Golden Gate Park, Redwood Regional — these are our classrooms.

What we actually do, week by week — Bay Area

Saturdays
Move & Mood Lab — 8:30am Golden Gate Park or Presidio community run + breathwork (free). HRV education, 30-minute talk on regulation science.
2nd Saturday
Trail Together — monthly hike, 5-8 miles, no-work-talk first hour, family-friendly, kids welcome. Rotates: Redwood Regional Park, Mission Peak, Rancho San Antonio, Los Altos Hills. Ends with tea + check-in.
Classroom
Classroom Calm — calm kits to 3 pilot Bay Area schools: box breathing cards, 5-4-3-2-1 posters, movement break timers, sleep hygiene sheets.
Ages 3-8
Little Wonderers — play-based emotional literacy for young children: story-based naming, feelings identification through play and parent co-regulation practices. No screens required.

What we do not do

  • We do not provide therapy, diagnosis, or crisis service. Educational only. If you need help now: 988 in US.
  • We do not sell supplements, courses, or retreats.
  • We do not scale with ads or data mining. We do not track you across sites.
