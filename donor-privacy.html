Why this page exists

A privacy policy tells you what we could do under law. A donor privacy promise tells you what we will never do, even if law would allow it. For a small family foundation in Bay Area, this is entire fundraising ethic.

This commitment aligns with Donor Bill of Rights — Association of Fundraising Professionals, AHP, CASE, Giving Institute. Your right to be informed, access records, assurance gift used as designated, and expectation personal information held in confidence.

Lifecycle of a gift — Bay Area, CA

  1. You initiate: On donate page, browser talks directly to Stripe over TLS 1.2+.
  2. Processor validates: Stripe fraud check, tokenized confirmation. Full card number never touches our servers.
  3. We receive minimal receipt data: Name, email, last 4 digits, exp month/year, amount, date, campaign, recurring status.
  4. We steward: Tax receipt for 501(c)(3) public charity, record in restricted ledger for IRS compliance, note communication preferences.
  5. We retain only as needed: 7 years for accounting/nonprofit law, then minimized.

What we store vs never store

What we keep
  • Full name and email for receipt
  • Mailing address if provided for tax receipt
  • Amount, date, frequency, designation
  • Last 4 digits + expiry from Stripe
  • Communication preference: email only, no mail, anonymous
What we never keep
  • Full PAN, CVV, bank account numbers
  • Government IDs, wealth data
  • Wealth screening scores
  • Location tracking, ad IDs
  • Donor data purchased from brokers
  • Children under 13 — programs through parents only

What we never do

  • No sale, no trade, no rental.
  • No sharing for marketing.
  • No mailings on behalf of others.
  • No prospecting.
  • No advertising lookalikes.
  • No re-identification of anonymous donors.
