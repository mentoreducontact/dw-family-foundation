DW Family Foundation is a small, science-rooted family foundation in the San Francisco Bay Area. We help modern people improve everyday mental well-being — what some call sub-health — before it becomes crisis. We work with workers, students, and young children (3-8) through three simple practices: move the body, restore attention in nature, and build emotional language together.

What is everyday sub-health? Not a clinical disorder. It is the gray zone before: poor sleep, afternoon crash, endless scrolling, irritability, rumination, Sunday scaries. In the US, 73% of adults report regular stress symptoms (APA 2023). We work here — early, local, and practical.

Inspired by Active Minds (peer support), The Jed Foundation (transitions), Mind.org.uk (science translation), and Child Mind Institute (early skills). We borrow what works, keep it local to the Bay.
01 / Move & Mood

Move to regulate

Weekly community runs, strength, and breathwork in Golden Gate Park and Presidio. We teach why 30 minutes of Zone 2 lowers cortisol 15-25% and supports BDNF — your brain fertilizer.

Zone 2HRVFor workers
02 / Trail Together

Restore attention outdoors

Monthly hikes in Redwood Regional Park, Mission Peak, Rancho San Antonio. Nature is not a backdrop — it is a protocol. 90 minutes in nature reduces rumination (Stanford 2015).

Redwood RegionalMission PeakFamily
03 / Little & Big Minds

Emotional literacy early

Little Wonderers (3-8) for play-based emotional literacy. Classroom Calm for exam stress. Work Well for tech burnout and manager training. Science, not slogans.
