Legal background — why a nonprofit has this page

Under California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by CPRA (California Civil Code Sections 1798.100 et seq.), nonprofit organizations are generally exempt from CCPA obligations. Donor and supporter personal information we collect in connection with charitable purpose is further addressed by Civil Code Section 1798.105(d)(7) & 1798.145 and related nonprofit exemptions (Sections 1798.100 et seq. nonprofit exemption) under California law.

In other words: law does not currently require DW Family Foundation, a 501(c)(3) public charity, to provide a “Do Not Sell or Share” opt-out. We provide it anyway as best practice, because standard we hold is higher than statute. Exact citation required for compliance clarity: §1798.105(d)(7) & §1798.145.

We drafted this to meet technical placement and content expectations of Cal. Code Regs. tit. 11 Section 7012 — clear, conspicuous link titled “Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information” in footer of every page.

What “sell” and “share” mean

  • Sell means exchanging personal information for monetary or valuable consideration, as defined by CCPA.
  • Share means sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising.

What we do not do

✦ We do not sell personal information for money. Never have.
✦ We do not share for behavioral advertising. No Meta pixel, no Google retargeting.
✦ We do not allow ad networks to collect data on our site.
✦ We do not upload donor emails to ad platforms.
