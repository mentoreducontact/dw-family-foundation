Legal • California Privacy

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information

DW Family Foundation does not sell personal information. We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. This page explains your rights under California law and how to exercise them.

California rights

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), California residents have rights to know, delete, correct, and opt-out of sale/sharing of personal information. We honor these rights.

Our practice: We do not sell or share personal information as defined by CCPA. We have no advertising trackers. Therefore, there is no sale or sharing to opt out from on this site. If this changes, we will add an opt-out mechanism and update this page.

For CCPA record-keeping, we reference that we do not sell or share donor or visitor data. Under CCPA civil code sections §1798.105(d)(7) & §1798.145, donor records retained for tax and nonprofit compliance are exempt from certain deletion requests, as disclosure or deletion would conflict with legal obligations.

How to exercise your rights

Email mentoredu.contact@gmail.com with subject "Privacy Request — [Know / Delete / Correct / Opt-out]". Include name and email you used with us. We verify by email reply. We respond within 45 days, with a possible 45-day extension with notice as allowed by law.

Non-discrimination

We will not discriminate against you for exercising privacy rights.
