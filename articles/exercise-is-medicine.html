Exercise as antidepressant: what moves when you move.

DW Foundation • Science & Stories•10 min read 运动即良药 • 内啡肽 • BDNF

If you could bottle the effect of a brisk walk, it would be a blockbuster drug with questionable patent prospects. Movement (运动) changes not just your body, but the chemistry that colors your mind. Not metaphorically — pharmacologically.

Five chemicals, one walk (一次快走，五种变化)

When you move at moderate intensity — fast enough to breathe harder, slow enough to talk (微喘可对话) — five systems respond within minutes to weeks:

  • Endorphins (内啡肽): The endogenous opioids. 20-30 minutes of continuous rhythmic movement lifts pain threshold and mood. Not the mythic "runner's high" only for marathoners — even gardening counts.
  • Serotonin (血清素): Exercise increases tryptophan transport into the brain and serotonin synthesis. This is part of why SSRIs and exercise converge on similar pathways, though via different routes. Regularity matters more than intensity.
  • Dopamine (多巴胺): Movement signals reward prediction. For sub-health states marked by flatness (提不起劲), dopamine restores motivation to start (启动力), not just pleasure at end.
  • BDNF — Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (脑源性神经营养因子): Think of BDNF as fertilizer (化肥) for neurons. It supports plasticity. Low BDNF is associated with stress and depression; exercise reliably raises it, especially with Zone 2 aerobic work and brief bursts.
  • Cortisol regulation (皮质醇调节): Chronic worry keeps cortisol high and flat — losing its healthy daily curve. Exercise, especially morning outdoor movement, restores a healthy spike-and-dip pattern, improving sleep and stress recovery (压力恢复).

What does the evidence actually say?

A quiet revolution happened in meta-analyses:

Effect size comparable to medication for mild-moderate depression. A 2024 BMJ meta-analysis of 218 trials (14,170 participants) found exercise had moderate effects on depression, with walking/jogging, yoga, and strength training among the top modalities. Dance had the largest effect but lowest adherence. Walking was best trade-off of effect + adherence.

30 minutes x 3 times per week works. Reckless intensity is not required. Brisk walking at 60-70% max heart rate — you can say short sentences — for 30-45 minutes, 3x/week for 8-12 weeks shows Cohen's d ~0.5-0.6 for mild-moderate symptoms, similar range to antidepressants for that severity (without implying replacement — many people use both).

Strength adds anti-anxiety value. Gordon et al., Sports Medicine 2018 meta-analysis of 33 RCTs: resistance training reduces anxiety symptoms (平均降低) regardless of strength gained. The mechanism isn't "looking better" — it's interoceptive: you learn your body can handle load.

Consistency beats heroics. The largest benefit is moving from zero to some. A 2020 JAMA Psychiatry study of 1.2M showed any movement above sedentary reduced depression risk; the steepest gain was the first 2.5 hours weekly.

Our prescription: Zone 2 + Strength, social by design

We translate science into a program called Move as Medicine — minimal gear, neighborhood scale, designed for parents who haven't slept.

Zone 2 base (有氧基础): 2-3x / week, 30-45 min at conversational pace. On flat: brisk walk 3.5-4.2 mph. On incline: slower. Use nasal breathing test — if you must mouth-breathe hard, ease off. Morning is ideal for cortisol curve, but any time beats none. With a child, stroller pace counts if incline.

Strength anchor (力量锚点): 2x / week, 20 min. 4 movements: push (俯卧撑/墙推), pull (弹力带划船), hinge (臀桥/硬拉), carry (抱娃负重走). 2-3 sets, 6-12 reps, leaving 2 reps in reserve. No gym required.

Social binder (社交粘合): Exercise adherence doubles when another person expects you. Our hikes pair people, not pace — you walk with one person, switch at turnaround. No leaderboard.

Practical Toolkit — A starter week (新手一周处方)

Move before you feel like it (先动再说)

Mon / Wed / Sat: 35-min walk. Route you know. Podcast off for first 10 min — notice 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you feel. That's attention training (注意力训练) disguised as warm-up.

Tue / Fri: 20-min strength at home. Pair with a habit: after coffee, before shower. Keep dumbbells visible (可见即行动).

Sleep pairing: No intense exercise within 3h of bed, but Zone2 walk after dinner helps glucose and sleep onset. Dim lights.

Track effort, not outcome: Check box "今日已动" rather than steps. Add one line: mood before / after 0-10. Most see +1 to +2 after.

When to pause: Chest pain, dizziness, new injury — stop and seek care. If mood worsens >2 weeks despite movement, please talk to clinician. Exercise is medicine, but not only medicine.

Safety: This is educational, not medical prescription. If you have cardiovascular history, pregnancy, or musculoskeletal issues, check with your clinician first.

Why we believe this for families

Parents often say they have no time to exercise because they are caring. Our reframe: exercise is caring — not away from family, but for family (为家人充电). A 30-minute walk where your prefrontal cortex recalibrates cortisol and BDNF is not selfish; it lowers irritability (易怒) and raises patience for the co-regulation your 5-year-old needs later.

We also like its honesty. No branding can fake heart rate. No affirmation replaces a hill. It is quiet luxury — time, breath, sweat.
