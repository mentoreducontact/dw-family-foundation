Exercise as a natural mood support:
endorphin, serotonin, dopamine, BDNF explained.

30 minutes of brisk walking 3 times a week shows mood effects comparable to other low-intensity interventions for mild-moderate low mood in meta-analyses. Here is what happens inside, told simply.

Cortisol — not villain, just misused fuel

Acute rise is useful — gets you out the door. Chronic elevation without movement completion is the problem. Moderate aerobic exercise metabolizes circulating cortisol, improves HPA axis feedback, and lowers evening cortisol that ruins sleep. Measure: better sleep latency in 2 weeks.

Endorphin and anandamide — immediate calm

20+ min rhythmic movement triggers endogenous opioids and endocannabinoids. Result: higher pain threshold, quieter self-criticism. Not runner's high every time — more like runner's calm.

Serotonin and dopamine — steady and motivated

Rhythmic aerobic activity plus daylight increases serotonin synthesis and sensitivity. Dopamine supports motivation to repeat tomorrow. That is why we pair group + same time — you do not need willpower, you need a time and place: Saturdays 8:30AM, Golden Gate Park.

BDNF — fertilizer for memory

Brain-derived neurotrophic factor rises most after 30+ min at 60-70% max heart rate. Supports hippocampal plasticity — the area sensitive to chronic stress. Translation: moving helps you remember and adapt, not just feel better for an hour.

TRY TOMORROW • BAY AREA

3 small practices

  • Walk 30 min in Golden Gate Park or Presidio — notice breath and light, no podcast.
  • One no-work-talk walk this week in Redwood Regional or Rancho San Antonio — phone on Do Not Disturb.
  • Name one feeling to one person without fixing: “I feel overwhelmed and I am going to take three breaths.”
