Little Wonderers • Play-based • Bay Area Libraries • Ages 3-8

Kids' big feelings are not bad behavior:
emotional literacy ages 3-8.

A 4-year-old who hits when frustrated is not misbehaving on purpose. They are experiencing a feeling that is bigger than their vocabulary and self-regulation. Our job as adults is to provide words, safety, and a calmer nervous system to borrow.

Naming is taming — brain basis

Labeling emotion engages prefrontal cortex and reduces amygdala reactivity. fMRI studies show affect labeling reduces threat. For kids, hearing an adult say calmly 'You are frustrated. Waiting is hard.' helps organize experience. Not permissive — it is informative.

Co-regulation, not control

Young children co-regulate: they borrow your calm. If you are dysregulated and shouting 'calm down,' they cannot. Our script: Pause, breathe visibly, name your own feeling, offer choices. 'I am feeling overwhelmed. I will take three breaths. Do you want to blow bubbles or squeeze a pillow?' You model what you want them to learn.

Bay Area play examples

Use redwoods: tall like redwoods when excited, small like acorn when tired. Use fog: 'You feel foggy?' Play in Presidio or local library: feelings matching cards 12 emotions, body map where feelings live, calm corner with pinwheel breathing. Free printable kit via mentoredu.contact@gmail.com.

What not to do

Do not shame, bribate with screens, or require immediate apology while flooded. Wait for calm. Teach repair after: 'You hit. Hitting hurts. Next time you can say I need space.' No child data collected — adults only.

TRY TOMORROW • BAY AREA

3 small practices

  • Walk 30 min in Golden Gate Park or Presidio — notice breath and light, no podcast.
  • One no-work-talk walk this week in Redwood Regional or Rancho San Antonio — phone on Do Not Disturb.
  • Name one feeling to one person without fixing: “I feel overwhelmed and I am going to take three breaths.”
