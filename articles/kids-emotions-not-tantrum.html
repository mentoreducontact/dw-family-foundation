When a 4-year-old shouts, hits, or collapses, it is often not defiance but overload. Skills help: co-regulation first (adult calm), then notice body cues, name feeling, do together. Example: notice tight fists, name 'I feel mad and hot', together push wall 5 seconds and breathe like smelling flowers. Repeating the same 30-second script across bath, car, and park builds interoception. Child Mind Institute research shows emotional literacy early predicts later coping. We are not therapists — we share play-based tools for everyday use.
