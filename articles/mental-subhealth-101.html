What is mental sub-health? The quiet in-between that deserves a name.

DW Foundation • Science & Stories•9 min read 心理亚健康自查 • Not a disorder, a signal

You sleep six hours and wake tired. You answer Slack, make dinner, help with homework — functional (功能正常), but thin. Not depressed, not anxious in the clinical sense, but frayed (心累) for weeks. In Chinese public health language, this state has a useful name: 心理亚健康 (xīnlǐ yà jiànkāng) — mental sub-health.

It is not a diagnosis. It is a place on the continuum. Think of health not as a binary — sick vs. well — but as a gradient from thriving to struggling to disorder. Sub-health lives in the middle: measurable distress, reduced recovery, no full criteria for DSM or ICD disorder. It is the body's early-warning system before the check-engine light becomes a breakdown.

Why we need a word for the middle

Western mental health systems are excellent at crisis and disorder. They are less practiced at naming the middle. The result: people wait. They tell themselves "it's not bad enough to bother someone" (还没严重到需要帮助). Research contradicts that instinct.

Prevalence is large, not niche. In China, repeated cross-sectional surveys of urban employees (城镇职工) report 60-75% endorse at least one sub-health symptom cluster for >1 month. In the U.S., CDC NHIS and Household Pulse data suggest ~22% of adults report anxiety or depressive symptoms that remain subthreshold, yet impair work and family life. Among parents of young children and tech workers logging 50+ hours, the number is higher.

Ignoring it has cost. Longitudinal work shows sub-health states predict later clinical episodes, sickness leave, and family conflict. Catching it early matters because interventions are lower-intensity, lower-stigma, and more effective when you still have some reserve.

Sub-health vs. clinical disorder: how to tell the difference

We are not clinicians, and this is not medical advice. Always seek professional evaluation if unsure. But conceptually:

  • Duration & pervasiveness (持续性与广泛性): Sub-health is intermittent and tied to context — worse after poor sleep, better after a weekend hiking. Clinical depression tends to be pervasive across domains for two weeks or more, even when circumstances improve.
  • Functioning (功能影响): In sub-health you function, but effortfully — "I can do it, but it costs me." In disorder, functioning often drops despite effort: missing deadlines, withdrawing, persistent anhedonia (快感缺失).
  • Reversibility (可逆性): Sub-health often shifts with rest, movement, connection, meaning — the levers we program for. Disorder often needs tailored clinical care, therapy, medication, or both.
  • Safety signals (安全信号): Any thoughts of self-harm, plan, intent, severe hopelessness, mania, psychosis, or inability to care for self/child requires immediate professional help — call 988 in US, Beijing 800-810-1117.

Think: sub-health is weather (天气), disorder is climate (气候). Weather deserves attention because sustained weather becomes climate.

The 8-item DW self-check (心理亚健康自查)

Use this not as a test, but as a conversation starter. Rate the last 2-4 weeks (过去2-4周): 0 = rarely, 1 = sometimes, 2 = often.

  • 01Emotional flatness or irritability (情绪低落/易怒): Small triggers, big reactions; joy feels muted.
  • 02Non-restorative sleep (睡眠不解乏): Tired after 7-8h, mind racing at 2am.
  • 03Rumination loop (反刍思维): Same worry on replay, hard to disengage.
  • 04Body tension (躯体紧张): Neck/shoulder tightness, headache, gut unease without medical cause.
  • 05Social pullback (社交退缩): Saying no to friends, preferring scrolling over seeing people.
  • 06Attention cost (注意力代价): Reading a page takes three tries; task switching feels expensive.
  • 07Coping by numbing (麻木应对): More caffeine, alcohol, night snacking, doomscroll to unwind.
  • 08Meaning thinness (意义感变薄): "What's the point?" not as philosophy but as fatigue.

Interpretation (仅作参考): 0-4 = likely thriving with normal stress. 5-9 = early signal — prioritize recovery this week. 10-12 = persistent sub-health — consider structured support (trail group, movement prescription, trusted conversation). 13+ or positive safety item = please reach to a clinician, counselor, or trusted person this week. We repeat: this is not diagnostic.

Practical tips — the 3-day reset (3日小重启)

When you score 5+, don't optimize — recover.

  • Sleep anchor (睡眠锚): Same wake time (±30m) for 3 days, no screens 45m before bed. Morning light 5-10 min.
  • One walk, one talk (一走一聊): 30-40 min brisk walk with one person, no headphones, 3x this week. Notice how rumination softens by minute 25.
  • Body before mind (先动身体): 10 minutes Zone 2 + 5 minutes stretch daily. Not performance — perfusion.
  • Micro-journal (微记录): Each evening, 3 lines: 一件小确幸 (small good), 一件小烦恼 (small strain), 一件明日可做的小事 (tiny next step). No analysis.
  • Ask for co-regulation (寻求共同调节): Tell one person: "我最近有点亚健康，可以每周一起走一次吗?" — make it concrete.

If symptoms persist >2 weeks or worsen, contact primary care or local counseling center. In US: 988 crisis line. In China: 800-810-1117 or local 精神卫生中心.

The gift of naming sub-health is permission to act early without identity shift. You don't need to become "a patient" to deserve rest, movement, nature, and better stories. You just need to heed the signal.
