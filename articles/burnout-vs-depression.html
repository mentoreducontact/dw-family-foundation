Burnout vs depression:
how to tell and what managers can do.

Burnout is chronic exhaustion + cynicism + inefficacy from unmanaged work stress. Depression crosses domains, includes anhedonia and hopelessness. Knowing the difference matters for what to do next.

WHO definition

Burnout is an occupational phenomenon, not a medical condition: exhaustion, mental distance/cynicism from job, reduced professional efficacy. Caused by chronic workplace stress not successfully managed. Not your personality flaw.

When it sounds like depression

Low mood across work and home, loss of pleasure in things you used to like, hopelessness, sleep/appetite change persisting 2+ weeks regardless of workload, thoughts of self-harm. If so, seek clinical evaluation promptly, not just vacation. US: call or text 988. We are educational only.

What helps burnout specifically

Recovery experiences: psychological detachment (not checking Slack), relaxation (walk Presidio), mastery (learn non-work skill), control (choose one boundary). Boundary scripts: 'I can do X Thursday if Y deprioritized, or Friday if X stays priority — which prefer?' Measure workload in numbers.

Manager checklist tomorrow

Cancel one recurring meeting permanently. Declare Wednesday morning no-meeting for focus and walk. Ask 'On 1-10, how overloaded this sprint?' not 'How are you?' Share your own boundary — 'I do not check Slack after 8pm.' Track after-hours pings. Provide EAP info without stigma.

TRY TOMORROW • BAY AREA

3 small practices

  • Walk 30 min in Golden Gate Park or Presidio — notice breath and light, no podcast.
  • One no-work-talk walk this week in Redwood Regional or Rancho San Antonio — phone on Do Not Disturb.
  • Name one feeling to one person without fixing: “I feel overwhelmed and I am going to take three breaths.”
