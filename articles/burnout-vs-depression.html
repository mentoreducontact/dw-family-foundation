Burnout vs. depression: same exhaustion, different address.

DW Foundation • Science & Stories•10 min read 职业倦怠 • 三维度 • Inspired by Bring Change 2 Mind

In tech Slack, burnout is a badge: "I'm burnt out" (我倦怠了) gets emoji. Depression gets silence. Understanding difference isn't to self-diagnose, but to choose right first step — recovery habit vs. clinical support — and to reduce shame (羞耻感) that keeps both hidden. Inspired by Mind (UK) and Bring Change 2 Mind (US) non-stigmatizing storytelling.

WHO definition: burnout is occupational, not medical

In ICD-11 (2019 inclusion), WHO defines burnout (QD85) as an occupational phenomenon (职业现象), not disease, resulting from chronic workplace stress not successfully managed. Three dimensions (三维度):

  • 1. Exhaustion (精疲力竭): depleted, tired, no bandwidth — "电池没电但还在亮着".
  • 2. Cynicism / depersonalization (疏离/愤世嫉俗): mental distance, negative feelings toward job, sarcasm as armor: "反正没人在乎".
  • 3. Inefficacy (效能感降低): feeling ineffective, stuck, "我是不是变菜了", reduced performance despite longer hours.

Key nuance: burnout is tied to role-context (工作情境绑定). You feel okay on hiking Saturday, dread Sunday 4pm when Slack preview pops. Depression tends to be pervasive (广泛性): less interest across domains, even things you used to love — music, kids, running — feel flat for weeks.

Overlap is real — and why it matters for tech

Studies of 10k+ tech workers show 60% overlap between high burnout scores and depressive symptoms (Maslach Burnout Inventory vs PHQ-9). Why tech specifically?

  • Create-release cycle without rest: Ship → retro → plan → ship. No season. Body keeps score.
  • Cognitive load + identity fusion: "What I build = who I am". Failed project feels like self failure (自我否定).
  • High autonomy, high ambiguity: Remote, async, always on — freedom without boundary (自由但无界) leads to self-exploitation.

Both burnout and depression share sleep disturbance, fatigue, irritability. Differential cues, not diagnostic:

When to seek help — a clear threshold (何时求助)

Seek professional evaluation promptly if: low mood / loss interest most days >2 weeks, thoughts "better off dead / not wanting to be alive", severe sleep/appetite change, inability to work/care for family, substance use to cope, or others worried about you. In US call/text 988, in Seattle King County Crisis 866-427-4747, China 800-810-1117. Seeking help is not career risk — untreated depression predicts more sick leave than early care.

For burnout sub-health (亚健康职业倦怠): Early action helps before it deepens. WHO framing says burnout is managed by both workplace and individual strategies. That leads to checklist.

Practical — Two checklists (为员工与管理者)

For you: The 7-day boundary reset (个人七日边界重启)

  • Audit load vs control (工作量与控制感): List top 3 energy drains. For each: can you reduce scope, increase control, or add resource? If none, that's systemic, not personal failing.
  • Hard edges (硬边界): No Slack after 7pm for 7 days. Status "after hours, will reply 9am". Put phone charger outside bedroom. 96% of burnout is boundary failure, not laziness.
  • Micro-recovery daily (每日微恢复): 30-min walk without podcast (see Trail Together), 8-min breathing before dinner transition, 1 non-work interaction where you are not "engineer" but person.
  • Meaning splice (意义切片): 90 min/week on task that restores competence — mentoring, fixing small bug that delights user — not just OKRs.
  • Say it out loud (说出来): Tell one colleague: "我最近有点倦怠，正在调整节奏。周五下午想一起走走吗?" Inspired by Bring Change 2 Mind — story reduces stigma (故事减少污名).

For managers: Burnout-aware team check (管理者清单)

Inspired by Mind's Manager Toolkit and our own practice as eng leads.

  • Measure energy, not just velocity: In 1:1 ask: "On scale 1-10, battery? What drained, what charged last week?" Track trend.
  • Model boundaries: No Sunday roadmap edits. Take hike, mention it. Your calendar is culture.
  • Clarify "good enough" (何为足够好): Many burnout comes from ambiguous excellence. Define done, not perfect.
  • Load visible: WIP limit 2, not 5. Say "no" to stakeholder so team doesn't have to.
  • Resource path: Know EAP, PTO policy, local counseling, and how to support return after leave without interrogation.
  • Language: Replace "we need to be more resilient" with "process may be burning people, let's adjust". Burnout is not resilience deficit (不是抗压能力差).

If your company wants a lunch walk or workshop, we do 45-min "Why hiking stops rumination" — peer led, no clinical claims, science grounded, Seattle parks.

Our stance: small, non-clinical, dignity-first

DWFF doesn't do therapy, EAP, or performance coaching. We do neighborhood practices that lower the floor: walk, move, tell better stories, name feelings. For burnout sub-health, those levers often shift exhaustion before it becomes clinical. For depression, they are adjuncts (辅助), not replacements, and reduce isolation while person seeks care.

Quiet luxury at work is not kombucha on tap. It's permission to leave laptop closed after 7pm and still be considered committed (有边界仍被认可为投入). That culture change is the real intervention.
