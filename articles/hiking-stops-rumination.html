Trail Lab • Redwood Regional Park • Rancho San Antonio • Bay Area Science

Why hiking quiets the inner monologue:
attention restoration in Bay Area redwoods.

Stanford found 90-min nature walk reduces rumination and subgenual prefrontal cortex activity vs urban walk. Our Trail Together design borrows directly from that protocol and from Attention Restoration Theory.

Rumination is sticky — nature unsticks

When you ruminate, brain's default mode network loops self-referential thoughts. Nature provides soft fascination — rustling leaves, fractal bark, distant fog — that gently captures attention without effort, allowing directed attention to restore. You cannot force it with willpower.

Bratman et al 2015 — what they did

38 participants with subclinical depression, random walk 90 min in grassland vs urban road. Nature walkers reported less rumination, showed decreased activity in subgenual prefrontal cortex on brain scan. Not huge sample, but replicated across studies with similar pattern.

How we design Trail Together

Hour 1 no work talk — to stop rehearsing emails. Hour 2 optional prompt about restoration — to practice noticing. Summit tea ritual — to anchor memory with warmth and community. Bay Area rotation: Redwood Regional Stream Trail for ease, Rancho San Antonio for oak savanna, Mission Peak for challenge, Presidio for fog and history.

Try without hiking far

If you cannot hike 90 min, try 20 min in Golden Gate Park Botanical Garden, no phone, notice 5 greens. Attention restoration is dose-dependent but starts early. Leave No Trace and respect trail closures for habitat.

TRY TOMORROW • BAY AREA

3 small practices

  • Walk 30 min in Golden Gate Park or Presidio — notice breath and light, no podcast.
  • One no-work-talk walk this week in Redwood Regional or Rancho San Antonio — phone on Do Not Disturb.
  • Name one feeling to one person without fixing: “I feel overwhelmed and I am going to take three breaths.”
