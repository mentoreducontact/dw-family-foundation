Foundation • 2026-07-21 • From TBD to Purpose

We started with legal pages. We found our purpose in the quiet in-between.

DW Foundation • Founders' Note•6 min read Mental Sub-Health 心理亚健康

When we scaffolded this site on GitHub Pages last July, the mission intentionally read TBD. Not from indecision, but from discipline. We wanted to build the scaffolding first — privacy, donor privacy (no sell/share/trade), state disclosures, audit trail — before we claimed a cause.

Over the past year, three threads kept converging in our own life as a young family in Seattle: Alan's work in tech where burnout (职业倦怠) is normalized, Jacqueline's circles with parents of 3-8 year olds who whisper "我是不是太焦虑了", and our kids podcast Daily Wonder where the most saved episodes weren't about dinosaurs, but about big feelings (情绪).

We started reading outside our lane: WHO's definition of burnout, Stanford's nature walk rumination study, Chinese literature on 心理亚健康 (mental sub-health) — that vast gray zone between thriving and diagnosis. In China, surveys suggest 70%+ of white-collar workers report sub-health states; in the US, the CDC reports 1 in 5 adults experience mental health symptoms that never meet full clinical criteria, yet degrade sleep, work, and relationships.

Not disease. Signal.

That phrase became our compass. Mental sub-health is not a disorder (不是病), it is a signal (是信号). It's the sleepless Sunday night, the hike you keep postponing, the 7-year-old who cannot name his frustration and calls it "stomach hurts." It's common, early, and remarkably responsive to low-intensity, human-scale interventions: walking together, moving together, storytelling together.

So we are re-chartering DW Family Foundation as a mental sub-health foundation. No clinic. No app to replace therapy. We fund and run small, neighborhood-scale practices that help families catch the whisper before it becomes a shout:

01 / Practice

Trail Together 结伴同行

Guided, slow, conversational hikes designed to break rumination (反刍思维) loops — attention restoration theory in practice, not theory.

02 / Movement

Move as Medicine 运动即良药

Zone 2 + strength prescriptions that act like antidepressants for mild-moderate states, with data from 30min x 3/wk studies.

03 / Stories

Little Wonderers 小小探索家

Emotional literacy for 3-8 year olds through story and co-regulation (共同调节) — an extension of Daily Wonder podcast.

We remain small by design. Family-funded, GitHub Pages hosted, every check published. We partner with organizations aligned with Mind and Bring Change to Mind principles — non-stigmatizing, evidence-informed, community-first.

If you have been in that in-between — functional but frayed (功能正常但心很累), present but preoccupied — this foundation is for you. Welcome.

What changes now

Legal scaffolding → Purpose-driven programs. Same GitHub history, new mission language across About, Programs, and Journal. EIN still pending 501(c)(3) — we will update transparency page when IRS determination arrives. No email lists sold, ever.
