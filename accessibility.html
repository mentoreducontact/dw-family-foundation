1. Our Commitment — WCAG 2.1 AA

DW Family Foundation is committed to providing a website that is accessible to the widest possible audience, regardless of ability, technology, or context. We aim to conform to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA, as published by the W3C Web Accessibility Initiative.

Accessibility is not a one-time checkbox for us; it is aligned with our mission. Psychological sub-health (心理亚健康) disproportionately affects those who are already excluded. If our journal about movement and mood cannot be read by a screen reader, we have missed the point.

This commitment applies to dwfamilyfoundation.org, our Jekyll static site hosted on GitHub Pages, including articles, program pages, transparency reports, and donation information pages.

2. Measures We Take

As a small team, we bake accessibility into our workflow rather than bolting it on:

◐
Semantic HTML & Landmarks
Proper <header>, <nav>, <main>, <footer> landmarks, heading hierarchy h1→h2→h3 without skipping, lists for lists, buttons for actions, links for navigation. No div-soup for primary structure.
A
Alternative Text
All meaningful images have alt text describing content and purpose — e.g., “family hiking in forest, calm.” Decorative images have empty alt. Unsplash photos retained with descriptive alt we write, not auto-generated. Future images we commission will include photographer + alt review.
⌨
Keyboard Navigation
All interactive elements — links, buttons, donation controls — are reachable by Tab, operable by Enter/Space, and show visible focus rings (outline: 2px solid var(--gold)). No keyboard traps. Skip-link pattern available via tabbing into header. We test with keyboard alone before shipping.
◑
Color Contrast & Typography
Our palette is chosen for contrast, not just luxury: cream #FFFCF8 background with ink #101010 text yields ~19:1 contrast. Forest #0F1912 header with #FFFCF8 text ~18:1. Gold #C8A96A is used only for eyebrow labels and accents above 18px or 14px bold, paired with forest or ink to meet 4.5:1. Body in Instrument Sans 16-18px, line-height 1.7, max 65ch measured.
↔
Responsive & Reflow
Mobile-first CSS grid, 320px to 2560px tested. Content reflows without horizontal scrolling. Tap targets minimum 44×44px. We avoid fixed viewport traps and ensure zoom to 200% retains readability without clipping.
🔊
Readable Language & Media
Clear language, limited jargon, Chinese/English side notes where helpful (心理亚健康 explained). No auto-play audio or video. Podcast episodes (future) will include transcripts. No flashing or rapidly blinking content that could trigger seizures.
✓
Forms & Donations
Donation flow via Stripe Checkout inherits Stripe’s accessible labeling. Any native forms we add will have associated <label> elements, fieldset, aria-describedby for errors, and server-side validation messages announced.

Header / Footer • Pass
Gold Accent

Tooling: We check with WebAIM Contrast Checker and Lighthouse Accessibility audit in Chrome DevTools on each deploy. Gold #C8A96A on cream #FFFCF8 alone is ~2.1:1 — we never use it for body text on cream; only on forest #0F1912 (4.8:1) or as graphic accent >3:1.

4. Compatibility & Assistive Technology

Our site is built to work with:

  • Latest versions of Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox on Windows, macOS, iOS, Android;
  • Screen readers: VoiceOver (macOS/iOS), NVDA and JAWS on Windows, TalkBack on Android;
  • Keyboard-only navigation and Switch devices;
  • Browser zoom up to 200% and OS text size adjustments without breaking layout.

We do not rely on JavaScript for core reading. The Site is static HTML/CSS; it should remain readable if JavaScript fails or is disabled, except Stripe checkout which requires JS for security.

5. Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some limitations can exist:

AreaLimitationOur Plan
Unsplash illustrative imagesSome photos from Unsplash (e.g., family hiking) may lack extended descriptions for complex scenes. Alt text is our own.Audit alt quarterly; add long descriptions for complex infographics (e.g., hormone charts) via caption or details element.
PDFs (future 990s, brochures)Scanned PDFs can be untagged.We will generate text-based PDFs, use heading structure, and offer HTML version of financials alongside PDFs.
Stripe CheckoutThird-party iframe; we cannot fully control its accessibility tree though Stripe maintains high AA coverage.Test with keyboard + screen reader; provide alternative donation by check if needed — email for instructions.
Color-coded pills & chartsPills using color alone could be ambiguous.We always add text label + icon; never color alone to convey meaning.

6. Feedback & Response

We welcome your feedback on accessibility barriers. Please include: the page URL, the issue, your assistive technology and browser, and what would help.

Email: mentoredu.contact@gmail.com
Subject line suggestion: “Accessibility feedback — [page]”
We aim to acknowledge within 48 hours and resolve low-effort fixes within 7 days, more complex within 30 days, and post updates in our Transparency log.

For those who prefer not to email: use our Contact form (when live) with same details, or write to: DW Family Foundation, Seattle, WA — Accessibility, and we will monitor GitHub Issues on our public repository if you report there.

7. Enforcement & Standards

We track our progress via:

  • Automated: Lighthouse Accessibility >=95 score target, axe-core checks in build (planned);
  • Manual: Keyboard-only walkthrough and VoiceOver spot checks for each new template;
  • Policy: No new page ships without alt review and contrast check documented.
